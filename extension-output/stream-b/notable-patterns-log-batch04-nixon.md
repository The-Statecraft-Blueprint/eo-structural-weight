## Batch 04 — Nixon (in progress)

### Chunk 1 (EO 11452-11480, 25 EOs) — complete

**PFIAB (EO 11460, 31.25%) — this chunk's most structurally significant order.** Establishes the President's Foreign Intelligence Advisory Board, revoking the pre-corpus EO 10938 (6.25%, `claude-church-bells-v1`). Flagged PRESENT on Accountability Gaps (reports only to the President, no external disclosure) and PRESENT on Preemption of Oversight, CRITICAL on Second/Third-Order Effects: in 1969, prior to the Church Committee reforms and the creation of any congressional intelligence oversight committee, this presidentially-appointed, presidentially-answerable-only Board was effectively the *sole* civilian check on the entire U.S. intelligence community. Scored strictly on structural/governance grounds, with no assessment of classified activity.

**EO 11472 (Environmental Quality Council, 12.5%) — direct institutional bridge to NEPA.** Terminates/revokes EO 11278 (Batch 3, 5.56%) and EO 11402 (Batch 3, 0.0%), consolidating recreation/natural-beauty functions into a new Council personally chaired by the President, paired with a 15-member Citizens' Advisory Committee. Notable: (1) Sec. 102(c) requires reports to *Congress*, a stronger accountability feature than most sibling coordinating councils this stream, which typically report only to the President (contrast with EO 11460 above); (2) Sec. 201 continues the confirmed staggered-cohort-term anti-capture design, the 4th instance after EO 11280/11287/11330 (all Batch 3); (3) historically short-lived — superseded within ~8 months by NEPA's statutory Council on Environmental Quality (Jan. 1970), making this order a genuine direct institutional ancestor of modern federal environmental governance.

**EO 11478 (Equal Employment Opportunity in the Federal Government, 18.75%) — foundational, enduring order, diff-aware parallel to EO 11246.** Supersedes Part I of EO 11246 (Batch 3, 27.27%, CRITICAL) and relevant portions of EO 11375 (Batch 3, coded by other coders, ~5%) as applied to federal employment specifically. Flagged CRITICAL on Second/Third-Order Effects purely for enduring decades-long structural reach, explicitly mirroring this project's neutral treatment of EO 11246 itself (flag reflects structural persistence, not policy endorsement). Otherwise well-designed: Sec. 4 creates a genuine internal-review-plus-CSC-appeal complaint process, a real accountability channel absent from several other bodies this chunk.

**IET family continues into the new administration (EO 11464, 22.22%).** Substantially lowers EO 11368's (Batch 3, 22.22%) rate table (max rate 18.75% → 11.25%), confirming this long-running family's template and scoring pattern survived the administration transition intact.

**Diff-aware correction to a prior pacing note.** EO 11476 (Manual for Courts-Martial, Revised edition, 6.25%) revises EO 11430 (Batch 3, 6.25%, issued Sept. 1968) after only ~9 months — correcting this stream's earlier suggestion (at EO 11430) that Manual reissuances follow a roughly 18-year interval. The Manual was, in fact, revised promptly when warranted.

**Two Council/committee establishments continue the Construction-clause coordinating-committee template unbroken across the administration transition:** EO 11452 (Council for Urban Affairs, 12.5% — also historically notable as a direct precursor to the statutory Domestic Council created in 1970) and EO 11470 (National Program for Voluntary Action, 6.25%, featuring a bounded HUD "seed money" grant authority).

**EO 11455 (Office of Intergovernmental Relations, 6.25%)** diff-aware supersedes EO 11426 (Batch 3, 6.25%), elevating the Federal-State liaison function from the OEP Director to the Vice President's direct supervision — a structural elevation, not a new risk.

**EO 11480 (President's Committee on Employment of the Handicapped, 12.5%)** — notable accountability gap: the Chairman holds unilateral, undefined at-will removal power over nearly all Committee members (Secs. 1(b), 3(a)), the only real structural concern in an otherwise routine reconstitution (supersedes EO 10994/EO 11018, both 0.0%).

**Routine templates confirmed carrying forward unbroken:** tax-inspection grants (EO 11454, 11457, 11461, all 5.56%, "direction matters" rule intact); EO 11248 salary-schedule additions (EO 11463, 11468, both 0.0%); EO 11157 incentive-pay amendments (EO 11473, the 6th tracked amendment, 0.0%); routine statutory pay adjustments (EO 11474/11475, both 6.25%, direct successors to EO 11413/11414).

### Chunk 2 (EO 11481-11506, 25 EOs) — complete

**The 1969 draft lottery (EO 11497, 33.33%, CRITICAL) — this chunk's most historically momentous order.** Implements the first U.S. military draft lottery since WWII, amending the decade-long Selective Service regulatory chain last touched by EO 11360 (Batch 3, 38.89%, CRITICAL). Flagged PRESENT on both Power Concentration and Accountability Gaps because the random-sequence methodology is left entirely to undefined presidential discretion ("as the President may direct") with no verification/audit procedure specified in the text — a purely structural read, arrived at independently of history, but notably validated by the well-documented statistical non-randomness of the actual December 1969 drawing (birth-date capsules were inadequately mixed). Also creates a defined married-registrant preference subgroup (Sec. 1631.7(a)(3)(iii)), flagged PRESENT on Exemptions Architecture.

**EO 11491 (Labor-Management Relations in the Federal Service, 33.33%) — foundational federal labor-relations code.** Diff-aware: formally revokes EO 10988 (Batch 2, Kennedy, 40.0%) and replaces it with a three-body structure (Federal Labor Relations Council, Federal Service Impasses Panel, Assistant Secretary of Labor for Labor-Management Relations) that directly foreshadows the Civil Service Reform Act of 1978's statutory FLRA. Scores somewhat lower than its predecessor (33.33% vs. 40.0%), plausibly due to its added structured appeal mechanism (Sec. 22) and clearer institutional role delineation. Main structural gap: Secs. 3(b)-(c) grant agency heads unreviewable "sole judgment" authority to exempt intelligence/security units and overseas installations from coverage — a genuine, if narrowly scoped, preemption of oversight.

**EO 11495 (Disaster Relief Act of 1969 Administration, 25.0%) — FEMA's structural ancestor.** Delegates the Act's grant/loan/housing authorities to the OEP Director with broad, largely undefined discretion over eligibility (Secs. 1(2), 1(8), 1(9)) and no specified compliance-monitoring mechanism — a direct structural precursor to the delegation framework later inherited by FEMA (1979).

**Paired urban/rural Cabinet-council architecture confirmed.** EO 11493 (Council for Rural Affairs, 6.25%) is the rural-policy mirror image of EO 11452 (Urban Affairs Council, Batch 4 chunk 1, 12.5%), sharing an identical Construction-clause template — the new administration built deliberate parallel urban/rural domestic-policy structures in its first year.

**D.C. National Guard command structure (EO 11485, 12.5%) — a useful structural contrast to the state-Guard-federalization template.** Unlike the 7 confirmed state-National-Guard-federalization instances from Batch 3 (always 20.0%, triggered by a state-to-federal command switch), the D.C. Guard has no governor and is always under direct federal control — this order is a command-and-control framework, not a federalization mechanism. Superseded EO 10030 (1949, pre-corpus, no score on record).

**Medal/award design contrast.** EO 11494 (Presidential Citizens Medal, 37.5%) has no nominating or vetting committee at all — purely unilateral, standardless presidential discretion ("at the sole discretion of the President," "exemplary deeds of service") — a genuine structural contrast with the National Medal of Science (EO 11287, Batch 3, 11.11%), which routes recommendations through a dedicated Committee with staggered terms.

**IET family narrows for the first time.** EO 11506 (16.67%) narrows (rather than expands) EO 11211's (Batch 3, 20.0%) Japanese-issues exclusion, as previously amended by EO 11368 (Batch 3, 22.22%) — the family's first tightening move rather than a rate-reduction or expansion.

**Routine templates confirmed continuing:** RLA Emergency Board (EO 11486, 0.0%); tax-inspection grants (EO 11483, 11505, both 5.56%, the latter another internal-security-committee grant continuing the "direction matters" rule); EO 11248 salary-schedule amendments (EO 11489, 11499, 11504, all 0.0%); routine succession/delegation orders (EO 11487 diff-aware supersedes EO 10753 [0.0%], 6.25%; EO 11498, 6.25%); EO 11501 (Foreign Military Sales administration delegation, 12.5%) — foundational State-Defense-Treasury FMS structure still recognizable today, with Sec. 2's explicit reinforcement of State's "continuous supervision" as a positive centralization-with-accountability feature.

### Chunk 3 (EO 11507-11535, 25 EOs) — complete

**EO 11534 (National Council on Organized Crime, 43.75%, CRITICAL) — this chunk's most structurally notable order, and one of the highest scores in the Nixon batch so far.** A single operative paragraph concentrates nineteen of the government's top law-enforcement/financial-regulatory officials (FBI, IRS, Customs, Secret Service, SEC, BNDD, INS, and more) into an Attorney-General-controlled Council with no reporting requirement, no Construction clause, no termination clause, and no defined procedures whatsoever — a striking contrast with this stream's other coordinating-council templates (EO 11452, 11472, 11493, 11526, all this batch), which uniformly include explicit non-encroachment clauses and enumerated functions. The sparseness itself, not the goal, drives the score.

**EO 11519 (Calling National Guard into Service, 38.89%, CRITICAL) — a historically significant escalation of the confirmed domestic-military-enforcement template.** Federalizes Guard units to respond to the 1970 postal wildcat strike — the first instance in this stream triggered by a domestic LABOR dispute rather than desegregation-enforcement or urban civil disorder (the two contexts confirmed across 7 instances in Batch 3, always 20.0%, e.g. EO 10730). Diff-aware comparison to EO 10730 revealed a genuine, meaningful accountability difference: EO 10730's action enforced a specific federal court's desegregation order (an independent judicial anchor), while EO 11519's trigger is solely the President's own unilateral characterization of the strike as "unlawful," with no independent branch determination. This weaker anchor plus the new triggering context justified scoring meaningfully above the established 20.0% template.

**EO 11507 (Federal Facility Pollution Control, 22.22%) — a comparatively well-checked exemption architecture.** Supersedes EO 11282 (20.0%) and EO 11288 (22.73%). Notable positive design: its Sec. 4(c) national-security/national-interest exemption mechanism requires periodic review AND mandatory reporting to CEQ — a stronger check than several other exemption architectures this stream has tracked, worth remembering as a comparison point.

**Industry vs. citizen environmental-advisory dual-track structure.** EO 11523 (National Industrial Pollution Control Council, 16.67%) — composed entirely of business/industry representatives with no counterbalancing public-interest members, flagged PRESENT on Third-Party Incentive Gaps — was established just weeks after EO 11472's citizen-only Environmental Quality Council/Citizens' Advisory Committee (Batch 4 chunk 1, 12.5%). Together they reveal a deliberate two-track (industry vs. citizen) environmental-advisory architecture at the formative moment just before NEPA's Council on Environmental Quality assumed the government's central environmental-policy role.

**Unstructured-medal-discretion theme recurs.** EO 11515's Sec. 5 (Terminating Certain Bodies, 25.0%) strips the 1945 Medal of Freedom order down to unstructured, standardless presidential discretion — a direct thematic echo of EO 11494's similarly uncommitteed Presidential Citizens Medal design (Batch 4 chunk 2, 37.5%). Two medal orders, two instances of removing/never-having any nominating committee.

**EO 11509 — an unusual reverse-sunset.** Explicitly strips away a predecessor's (EO 11353, 0.0%) time limitation on its cost-reduction function ("without regard to the time limitation contained therein") — the reverse of this stream's more common pattern of adding sunsets, worth tracking as a rare counter-example.

**EO 11513 (President's Commission on School Finance, 0.0%)** confirms this stream's positive well-bounded-study-commission template (clean 2-year deadline + 30-day wind-down) continues cleanly into the Nixon administration, matching the Warren/Kerner/Eisenhower Commission pattern from Batch 3.

**Routine templates and consolidations confirmed:** EO 11248 salary-schedule amendments (EO 11510, 11516, both 0.0%); EO 11157 amendments (EO 11511, the 7th tracked amendment, 0.0%); routine pay adjustments (EO 11524/11525, both 6.25%, successors to EO 11474/11475); tax-inspection grant (EO 11535, 5.56%, historically notable as the Douglas-impeachment-investigation grant, still scored per the standard "direction matters" template); housekeeping terminations (EO 11515, EO 11529, continuing the EO 11408/EO 11437 pattern); EO 11382 "DOT Reorganization Sweep" touched again (via EO 11515 Sec. 1(c)), continuing its status as this stream's most frequently re-touched hub order; EO 11533 (Export Administration Act administration, 18.75%) — foundational ancestor of the modern Commerce Dept. export-control (BIS) structure, with genuinely retained oversight (mandatory Board referral + presidential reporting).

### Chunk 4 (EO 11536-11561, 25 EOs) — complete: the "1970 EOP Reorganization Wave"

**EO 11541 (OMB / Domestic Council, 43.75%, CRITICAL) — one of this stream's most historically significant orders to date.** Implements Reorganization Plan No. 2 of 1970: creates the Office of Management and Budget (replacing the Bureau of the Budget, continuous since 1921) and formally establishes the Domestic Council, consolidating and terminating three prior Cabinet coordinating councils — EO 11452 (Urban Affairs), EO 11472/11514 (Environment), EO 11493 (Rural Affairs), all this batch. Two genuine diff-aware losses identified: (1) EO 11472's unusual Sec. 102(c) congressional-reporting requirement (flagged as a positive differentiator in chunk 1) does NOT carry forward into the Domestic Council; (2) none of the three predecessors' explicit Construction (non-encroachment) clauses — a consistent feature of this stream's coordinating-council template all administration — survived the consolidation either. A landmark, still-operative reorganization with two identifiable, real accountability losses baked into its creation.

**EO 11556 (Office of Telecommunications Policy, 25.0%) — a second major 1970 reorganization, and EO 11548 (Water Pollution Control Act delegation / EPA-transition anticipation, 12.5%) — a third.** Together with EO 11541, these three orders (all in this single chunk) reveal a genuine, concentrated burst of Executive Office of the President restructuring activity in mid-1970: OMB/Domestic Council (July 1), water-pollution/EPA-transition delegation (July 20), and OTP (September 4) — worth remembering as a cluster when navigating this period. EO 11556 notably amends EO 11051 (this stream, 55.56% — this stream's highest score touched anywhere in this chunk) and preserves a genuine positive check (Sec. 14's explicit FCC-authority-preservation clause).

**Vice-Presidential-chairmanship pattern confirmed a third time.** EO 11547 (Youth Opportunity Council) and EO 11551 (National Council on Indian Opportunity) both restructure existing coordinating bodies under VP chairmanship, joining EO 11455 (Intergovernmental Relations, chunk 1) — a recurring administration-wide organizational pattern of routing domestic-coordination chairmanships through the Vice President. EO 11551 also has a genuine positive design feature: fixed two-year terms for its Indian-leader members, providing structured tribal representation rather than pure at-will appointment.

**A rare, well-designed built-in sunset.** EO 11554 (0.0%) explicitly suspends a Navy promotion-percentage cap "for a period of two years" rather than indefinitely — a positive counter-example worth remembering alongside this stream's more common indefinite-suspension pattern.

**Historically notable but structurally neutral findings, evaluated consistently with this stream's template-fidelity convention:** EO 11536 (Scranton Commission on campus unrest, 0.0%) — cleanly bounded despite its historically fraught subject (Kent State/Jackson State); EO 11546 (UN 25th Anniversary Commission, 0.0%) — notable for including sitting Senators/Representatives as voting members, a genuine cross-branch feature; EO 11555 (16.67%) — extends Selective Service local-board eligibility to women for the first time in this stream's chain, a modest positive civic-inclusion development scored on neutral structural grounds.

**Routine templates confirmed continuing:** EO 11248 salary-schedule amendments (EO 11540, 11542, 11550, all 0.0%, with EO 11540 notable as the first textual OMB-renaming evidence); RLA Emergency Boards (EO 11543, 11558, 11559, all 0.0%); meat-import delegation (EO 11539, 6.25%, notably well-checked via mandatory multi-party concurrence); ceremonial/medal orders (EO 11544 Vice Presidential Service Certificate, EO 11545 Defense Distinguished Service Medal — the latter notable for its SecDef-regulation-bounded design, a more structured contrast to this stream's unilateral-discretion civilian medal findings).

### Chunk 5 (EO 11562-11586, 25 EOs) — complete: reorganization wave continues + a labor-emergency contrast pair

**1970 Reorganization Wave grows to 5 confirmed pieces.** EO 11564 (Transfer of Programs to Commerce, 12.5%) implements Reorganization Plan No. 4 of 1970, one of the founding orders establishing NOAA by consolidating oceanic/atmospheric functions from Navy, Army Corps, and Coast Guard — joining EO 11541 (OMB/Domestic Council), EO 11548 (EPA-transition), and EO 11556 (OTP), all Batch 4 chunk 4. EO 11579 (Overseas Private Investment Corporation, 12.5%) is historically significant in its own right and also diff-aware continues the ongoing multi-year disaggregation of EO 10973 (this stream's confirmed "hub-order echo," Batch 3, 20.0%) — a further piece touched as late as 1971.

**A genuine, well-matched diff-aware contrast: two domestic-labor-emergency tools, two very different accountability designs.** EO 11572 (Railroad Strike Transportation Priorities, 12.5%) responds to the December 1970 signalmen's strike using Defense Production Act priorities/allocation authority with an explicit, self-executing sunset (strike resolution, capped at 15 additional days) — a genuinely better-bounded design than EO 11519 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 3, 38.89%, CRITICAL), which federalized the National Guard for the same era's postal strike with indefinite duration and no independent anchor. Worth remembering as a clean comparison point: same administration, same year, same general problem (a transportation-adjacent strike), very different structural risk profiles depending on which tool was chosen.

**EO 11570 (Postal Rate Commission ethics regulations, 6.25%) — a genuine good-governance model.** Public ex parte-contact records (naming individuals and subject matter), dual-track financial disclosure (Commission + CSC), and explicit gift/honoraria prohibitions for a new quasi-judicial rate-setting body — one of this stream's more robustly designed ethics frameworks, worth keeping as a positive comparison point for future ethics-related orders.

**EO 11574 (Refuse Act Permit Program, 12.5%) — historically significant as the direct ancestor of the modern NPDES permit system** (Clean Water Act, 1972), with a genuinely strong positive design feature: the permitting agency (Army) is textually bound to accept the EPA Administrator's water-quality determinations (Sec. 2(a)(2)(A)) — a real, binding external check rarely stated this explicitly in this stream.

**EO 11575 (Disaster Relief Act of 1970 Administration, 20.0%) scores below its predecessor EO 11495 (25.0%, Batch 4 chunk 2) for a genuine, identifiable reason:** Sec. 1(a) reserves the major-disaster-declaration trigger and other consequential determinations to the President personally rather than delegating everything to the OEP Director — a real, if partial, accountability improvement, even though the same broad grant/loan discretion and absent compliance-monitoring mechanism persist for whatever remains delegated.

**EO 11583 (Office of Consumer Affairs, 12.5%) and EO 11562 (Physical Fitness reconstitution, 6.25%) both include genuine continuity-preservation clauses** during reorganization (existing Council members keep their original terms) — consistent with this stream's recurring positive "preserve sitting members during consolidation" pattern (EO 11472, Batch 4 chunk 1).

**Long-running-lineage pattern extended again.** EO 11571 confirms the DC Alley Dwelling Authority lineage (first tracked at EO 11401, Batch 3) is now a 7-link, 36-year-plus continuous statutory chain running from 1934 to 1970.

**Named-individual civil-service exemption template — third confirmed instance.** EO 11568 (A. Everette MacIntyre, FTC) matches this stream's established 5.56% template exactly.

**Routine templates confirmed continuing:** RLA Emergency Boards (EO 11585, 0.0%); tax-inspection grant (EO 11584, 5.56%); EO 11248 amendments (EO 11581, 0.0%); routine pay adjustments (EO 11576/11577, both 6.25%, under the newly-enacted Federal Pay Comparability Act of 1970); river-basin planning commission template (EO 11578, Ohio River Basin Commission, 6.25%, matching EO 11528's positive Governor-concurrence/multi-level-reporting design); Selective Service technical refinements (EO 11563, 11569, 11586) continuing to layer onto the EO 11497 lottery system without independently landmark findings.

### Chunk 6 (EO 11588-11613, 25 EOs) — complete: two of the stream's highest-scoring orders yet

**EO 11605 (Amending EO 10450 security-requirements framework, 43.75%, CRITICAL) — one of the most structurally serious findings in this entire stream.** Elaborates the Subversive Activities Control Board's power to designate organizations "totalitarian," "fascist," "communist," or "subversive," disqualifying individuals from federal employment via mere organizational "sympathetic association," with no described individual hearing right and no independent check on the designation process. Amends EO 10450 (this stream, 65.0% — already one of the highest-scoring orders in the whole stream). Historically notable that the underlying Board would be defunded by Congress roughly two years later (1973). Evaluated strictly on structural grounds (concentration + vague ideological categories + absent review), explicitly without commentary on anti-communism or any political viewpoint, consistent with this project's neutral-evaluation convention applied throughout.

**EO 11588 (Construction Industry Stabilization Committee, 40.91%, CRITICAL) — the pilot for the entire Nixon wage-price-control apparatus.** The first sectoral mandatory wage-review body under the Economic Stabilization Act of 1970, directly modeling the Cost of Living Council/Pay Board/Price Commission structure Nixon would launch economy-wide just months later (the August 1971 "Nixon Shock"). Genuine structural findings: a self-policing-first-instance compliance design (craft dispute boards composed of the regulated parties themselves get automatic finality if the government Committee misses a 15-day review window); an easily-gamed backward-looking wage benchmark (1961-1968 settlement averages); a centralizing certification-triggers-cross-government-contract-denial enforcement mechanism in the Secretary of Labor; and an undated non-enforcement window for the parallel Interagency Committee's price/compensation criteria.

**EO 11610 (Contractor indemnification for hazardous/nuclear risk, 27.27%) — a genuine, well-reasoned moral-hazard finding.** Government indemnification of defense contractors explicitly covers ordinary negligence (not just no-fault risk) for "unusually hazardous or nuclear" work — the same broader risk-shifting logic as the Price-Anderson Act's nuclear-liability framework, applied to defense contracting generally. Partially bounded by a willful-misconduct exclusion and mandatory financial-protection assessment, but the underlying safety-incentive concern is real.

**EO 11602 (Clean Air Act contract/grant/loan debarment, 16.67%) — a positive exemption-transparency model.** Still the operative facility-debarment mechanism under the Clean Air Act today, notable for Sec. 8(c)'s mandatory ANNUAL congressional reporting of every exemption granted or in effect — one of this stream's stronger exemption-transparency designs, worth remembering as a comparison point against weaker exemption regimes.

**EO 11599 (Special Action Office for Drug Abuse Prevention, 18.75%) — founding order of the modern federal drug-control bureaucracy** (direct ancestor of today's ONDCP), notable for its explicit, deliberate exclusion of law-enforcement activities from the Director's authority at founding — a treatment/coordination-focused mandate structurally distinct from how the "War on Drugs" would later be framed.

**Two railroad strikes, same well-designed template.** EO 11594 (May 1971) confirms the clean, self-executing DPA transportation-priorities sunset first seen at EO 11572 (December 1970, Batch 4 chunk 5) is a genuine repeatable administration-wide template, not a one-off.

**EO 11606 continues the draft-lottery audit gap.** Formalizes the lottery drawing into a standing annual procedure without ever specifying an audit/verification standard for "impartial" randomization — the same structural gap first identified at EO 11497 (Batch 4 chunk 2, CRITICAL), now a permanent feature of the annual process.

**Positive design notes:** EO 11590 extends ethics (EO 11222) and EEO (EO 11478) coverage to the newly independent Postal Service/Postal Rate Commission, closing a potential coverage gap from the 1970 Postal Reorganization Act. EO 11607's Advisory Council on Intergovernmental Personnel Policy reports to both President AND Congress with a bounded 18-month initial-report deadline. EO 11612 extends OSHA-style protections to federal employees with the latest confirmed instance of this stream's staggered-cohort-term anti-capture pattern (Federal Safety Advisory Council).

**Reorganization-sweep template repeats.** EO 11609's 21-subsection consolidated delegation (GSA/Treasury/HEW/State/Defense/VA/CSC/OMB) matches the "DOT Reorganization Sweep" (EO 11382, Batch 3) precedent: enormous touch-count, but each component narrowly defined, so not scored as improper bundling.

**Routine templates confirmed continuing:** tax-inspection grants (EO 11611, 5.56%, notably the renamed HUAC-successor "House Committee on Internal Security"); international-org designations (EO 11596, 16.67%, matching EO 11484's template); EO 11248 amendment (EO 11604, 0.0%); EO11157 amendment (EO 11591, the 8th tracked, 0.0%); ACTION agency creation (EO 11603, 18.75%) diff-aware absorbing EO 11470's Voluntary Action Program.

### Chunk 7 (EO 11614-11641, 25 EOs) — complete: the Phase II wage-price-control apparatus arrives

**EO 11627 (Further Providing for the Stabilization of the Economy, 36.36%, CRITICAL) and EO 11640 (same title, statutorily-finalized version, 31.82%, CRITICAL) — very likely the single most historically significant pairing in this batch to date.** These establish and then finalize the Cost of Living Council/Pay Board/Price Commission apparatus for Nixon's Phase II wage-price controls, the most sweeping peacetime economic controls since WWII (following the initial 90-day freeze, EO 11615, coded by other coders at 31.82%-50.0%). Genuine structural findings: an extraordinary delegation of the President's ENTIRE Economic Stabilization Act authority to a sub-Cabinet Council; Pay Board/Price Commission members serving at the President's pleasure with no independent tenure; a textually explicit asymmetry between mandatory wage/price controls and merely-voluntary interest/dividend restraints (sharpened, not resolved, in EO 11640); and a broad, largely standardless multi-layered exemption architecture. **Genuine diff-aware finding: EO 11640 scores LOWER than its predecessor EO 11627** due to two identifiable structural improvements when the Economic Stabilization Act Amendments of 1971 were incorporated — Pay Board/Price Commission members now require Senate confirmation (Secs. 7(b)/8(b)), and Sec. 15(d) cleanly abolishes the Interagency Committee on Construction outright, resolving the CISC/Pay-Board jurisdictional ambiguity flagged in EO 11627 rather than leaving it unresolved. Both evaluated strictly on governance/structural design, without commentary on wage-price-control economic policy itself.

**EO 11639 (Pay Adjustments for Federal Prevailing Rate Systems, 18.75%) — a genuine second/third-order-effects finding.** Directly subordinates ordinary federal civil-service "prevailing rate" wage administration to the Pay Board's guidelines from EO 11627/11640 — confirming the Phase II control apparatus's reach extended into the government's own internal pay-setting, not just the private economy.

**Dual-track "War on Drugs" institutional split confirmed complete.** EO 11641 (Office for Drug Abuse Law Enforcement, 18.75%) is the formal enforcement counterpart to EO 11599's (Batch 4 chunk 6, 18.75%) treatment-focused Special Action Office for Drug Abuse Prevention, with Sec. 2 mandating coordination between the two Directors — a deliberate dual-track design, not consolidation.

**Industry-vs-citizen advisory-body pattern extended to consumer affairs.** EO 11614 (National Business Council for Consumer Affairs, 11.11%) is an industry-only advisory body — the same third-party-incentive-gap structure as EO 11523 (National Industrial Pollution Control Council, Batch 4 chunk 3, 16.67%) — notably advising the FTC and DOJ, agencies with direct enforcement authority over the same member businesses. Parallels the earlier EO 11472 (citizen-only) vs. EO 11523 (industry-only) environmental-council split, and pairs with EO 11583 (Office of Consumer Affairs, Batch 4 chunk 6, 12.5%) as the citizen-facing counterpart.

**First-ever deviation from the named-individual civil-service-exemption template's clean sunset.** EO 11619 (Rose A. Conway, Truman's former personal secretary, 11.11%) is the 4th confirmed instance of this stream's named-individual exemption template but the first with an INDEFINITE duration rather than the template's usual clean, date-bounded sunset (contra EO 11568 MacIntyre, Batch 4 chunk 5, 5.56%) — scoring modestly higher as a result.

**Positive procedural-safeguards contrast to EO 11605.** EO 11633 (NATO/SEATO/CENTO security clearance program, 6.25%) explicitly incorporates EO 10865's (this stream, 30.0%) established hearing/appeal-type procedural safeguards by reference, and its preamble expressly acknowledges privacy/freedom concerns — a notably more due-process-conscious design than EO 11605's (Batch 4 chunk 6, 43.75%, CRITICAL) Subversive Activities Control Board mechanism, which described no individual hearing right at all for a comparably serious loyalty/security designation.

**Transitional Okinawa-reversion context.** EO 11618 (Further Amending EO 10713, Ryukyu Islands, 12.5%) extends local officials' terms pending the U.S.-Japan Reversion Agreement's entry into force (effective May 1972) — administrative wind-down of a 27-year U.S. military administration. Predecessor EO 10713 remains one of this stream's highest-scoring orders (70.0%), reflecting the inherent structural weight of foreign military administration.

**New helper template documented: LMRA/Taft-Hartley Board of Inquiry (`loi_board()`).** EO 11621 (West Coast dock strikes, maritime industry, 6.25%) confirms this template (previously used in Batch 3, not yet re-implemented as a Batch 4 helper until this chunk) — distinguished from the clean, self-executing RLA Sec. 10 Emergency Board template by an indefinite-continuation clause requiring the Board to remain available for further functions after its report.

**Routine templates confirmed continuing:** tax-inspection grants (EO 11624, EO 11631, both 5.56%); EO 11248 amendment (EO 11634, 0.0%); river-basin commission continuation with clean multi-party-request extension clause (EO 11635, Souris-Red-Rainy, 0.0%, matching EO 11528/11578's positive design); routine statutory pay adjustments (EO 11637/11638, both 6.25%, direct successors to EO 11576/11577).

**Two mid-chunk verification fixes applied directly to the batch JSON** (self-referential percentage errors caught by the standard post-hoc regex check, not re-run through code_and_append): EO 11614's notes originally misstated its own score as 16.67% (actual 11.11%); EO 11621's `loi_board()` template notes defaulted to the old 5.56% RLA-adjacent figure but this order's actual computed score is 6.25% (also corrected in EO 11622's cross-reference to EO 11621).

### Chunk 8 (EO 11642-11667, 25 EOs) — complete: the modern classification system arrives

**EO 11652 (Classification and Declassification of National Security Information and Material, 35.0%, CRITICAL) — the foundational modern U.S. classification-system order.** Direct structural ancestor of every subsequent classification EO this stream will eventually code (Carter's EO 12065, Reagan's EO 12356, Clinton's EO 12958, Obama's EO 13526) — flagging prominently now for cross-referencing when those later orders are reached. Genuinely strong, positive design features: an automatic 6/8/10-year downgrading schedule by classification level, a mandatory 30-year absolute declassification cap, a public/interagency mandatory-review-on-request mechanism after 10 years, and explicit textual prohibition on classifying to conceal error or avoid embarrassment (Sec. 4). These are meaningfully undercut by weak, self-policed enforcement against over-classification (Sec. 13's reprimand-only sanction, administered by the very Department that over-classified) and a layered, if bounded, exemption architecture from the declassification schedule itself. Issued less than a year after the Pentagon Papers litigation and roughly two years ahead of the Watergate-era classification/executive-privilege disputes that would test this exact framework — evaluated strictly on structural design, without commentary on any specific classification decision.

**EO 11647 (Federal Regional Councils, 31.25%) — a significant, previously untracked extension of the '1970 EOP Reorganization Wave'** (EO 11541 OMB/Domestic Council, Batch 4 chunk 5, 43.75% CRITICAL). **First-ever confirmed "reversion-to-predecessor" pattern.** EO 11661 (12.5%) explicitly revokes an intervening amendment (EO 11260, 1965) and reverts wholesale to an earlier order's (EO 11185, 1964) original federal-education-coordination provisions — structurally distinct from every amendment chain tracked so far in this stream, which have all been forward/accretive. Worth watching for recurrence in later administrations.

### Chunk 9 (EO 11668-11693, 25 EOs) — complete: two "Congress fixes the executive stopgap" pairings in one chunk, plus the first clear zombie-emergency-trap trigger

**EO 11695 ("Phase III" of the Economic Stabilization Program, 40.91%, CRITICAL) — one of the batch's most important diff-aware findings.** Widely understood historically as a *loosening* of Nixon's wage-price controls toward voluntary compliance, but structurally this order is a genuine ACCOUNTABILITY REGRESSION relative to its immediate predecessor: it abolishes the Senate-confirmed Pay Board and Price Commission (the very improvement flagged as positive at the EO 11627->EO 11640 transition, Batch 4 chunk 7) and re-concentrates all delegated Economic Stabilization Act authority in a single, non-confirmed Cost of Living Council Chairman. ### Chunk 12 (EO 11746-11770, 25 EOs) — complete: the Federal Energy Office arrives amid the OPEC embargo

### Chunk 1 (EO 11798-11824, 25 EOs) — complete: the transition, the clemency program, and a post-Watergate self-limiting reform

**This chunk opens exactly where Batch 4 closed: Ford's very first Executive Order.** EO 11798 (10.0%), issued August 14, 1974 -- six days after Nixon's resignation -- revokes a two-week-old Nixon order and re-anchors export-control administration to EO 11533 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 1, 18.75%) under freshly-enacted congressional legislation (PL 93-372), structurally identical to the EO 11677/EO 11683 zombie-emergency-resolution pattern (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 9). **A second, near-identical instance appears later in this same chunk**: EO 11818 (10.0%, November 5), continuing EO 11533 again under yet another fresh statute (PL 93-500) -- revealing a recurring bimonthly "TWEA export-control legislative patching" cycle during the fall 1974 transition period, worth tracking as this stream continues into 1975.

**The Vietnam-era clemency program — one of Ford's defining legacies — arrives as a paired structure.** EO 11803 (Presidential Clemency Board, 12.5%) and EO 11804 (Selective Service Director's alternate-service administration, 12.5%), both issued September 16, 1974, implement Ford's conditional-amnesty Proclamation for Vietnam-era draft evaders and military deserters (ultimately covering roughly 15,000 individual cases). EO 11803 has a genuine, clean, self-executing Dec. 31, 1976 sunset (Sec. 9) but a real accountability gap: no described public transparency, term limits, or removal standard for its 9 presidentially-appointed members. EO 11804's Selective Service Director administers the companion alternate-service program under his own self-prescribed regulations -- a real, if narrow, vague-enforcement-standard finding. Both evaluated strictly on structural/procedural grounds, independent of the underlying amnesty policy, consistent with this project's neutral-evaluation convention.

**A genuinely positive, self-limiting post-Watergate reform.** EO 11805 (Inspection of Tax Returns by the President and White House Staff, 12.5%) has Ford voluntarily TIGHTENING (not expanding) his own staff's access to taxpayer data -- requiring his own personal written signature for any request, naming designees individually, and prohibiting further disclosure without written presidential direction. A direct, deliberate response to well-documented Nixon-era IRS-access abuses, and a structural ancestor of the Tax Reform Act of 1976's stricter Sec. 6103 taxpayer-privacy provisions. One of the clearest examples in this stream of a President structurally constraining his own office rather than expanding it.

**The energy-office restructuring saga crosses into a new administration, and a new statutory footing.** EO 11814 (Activation of the Energy Resources Council, 12.5%) is the fifth link in this stream's tracked energy-office sequence (EO 11712 -> EO 11726 -> EO 11748 -> EO 11775, all Nixon, this stream -> this order, Ford), implementing the Energy Reorganization Act of 1974. Diff-aware: scores meaningfully LOWER than EO 11790 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 13, 38.89% CRITICAL) or EO 11748 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 12, 37.5% CRITICAL) because it replaces an informal, undefined Nixon-era ad hoc committee with a properly statutory, congressionally-authorized coordinating body -- even though its own membership spans nearly the entire executive branch. EO 11819 (12.5%), five weeks later, further builds out the Council's membership, matching this stream's established "modest positive tweak to an already-scored parent body" template (cf. EO 11714, this stream, 12.5%).

**A direct structural ancestor of the modern federal regulatory-review apparatus.** EO 11821 (Inflation Impact Statements, 18.75%) requires OMB-administered inflation-impact certification for all major executive-branch legislative/regulatory proposals -- flagged prominently now, as this is the direct structural ancestor of Reagan's EO 12291 (1981), Clinton's EO 12866 (1993), and Obama's EO 13563 (2011), all still to come in this stream. Genuine vague-enforcement-standard concern (undefined evaluation criteria) offset by an explicit, clean Dec. 31, 1976 sunset.

**A genuinely balanced advisory body, worth remembering as a positive counter-example.** EO 11809 (President's Labor-Management Committee, 6.25%) has equal 8-labor/8-management representation plus a neutral coordinator -- a notable structural contrast to this stream's recurring industry-only-advisory-council pattern (EO 11523, EO 11614, EO 11695, EO 11753, all this stream, Nixon).

**A useful correction to a "cleanest sunset" claim.** EO 11806 (0.0%) extends EO 11763's (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 12, 0.0%, previously flagged as "arguably the cleanest self-executing sunset in the entire stream") World Population Year Commission life from its original calendar-year-1974 bound to a new June 30, 1975 date -- even the cleanest-looking sunsets should be treated as provisional until the date has actually passed.

### Chunk 2 (EO 11825-11850, 25 EOs) — complete: the Rockefeller Commission and a major trade-authority consolidation

**The Rockefeller Commission — one of this stream's most historically significant orders.** EO 11828 (Commission on CIA Activities Within the United States, 12.5%), established January 4, 1975, was Ford's direct response to Seymour Hersh's December 1974 New York Times exposé of CIA domestic surveillance (Operation CHAOS and related programs). A genuine, real accountability gap (no public-reporting requirement for the Commission's own findings) is offset by a clean, tight sunset (Sec. 6: final report within three months, termination one month after) and a mandatory Attorney-General-referral requirement for any criminal evidence uncovered. Direct structural predecessor to Ford's own later EO 11905 (1976, still to come in this batch) and to the permanent congressional intelligence oversight committees (SSCI/HPSCI) created in 1976-77. **Its own sunset needed extending too**: EO 11848 (0.0%), two months later, extends the reporting deadline to a fixed date — consistent with this stream's now well-documented pattern that even tight, well-designed sunsets are frequently extended in practice (cf. EO 11806, EO 11837/EO 11842, all this batch).

**A major trade-authority consolidation, and this chunk's highest score.** EO 11846 (Administration of the Trade Agreements Program, 31.25%) comprehensively consolidates Trade Act of 1974 authority in the Special Representative for Trade Negotiations — direct structural ancestor of the modern USTR (formalized 1980). Two genuine, real findings drove the elevated score: Sec. 2(f)'s explicit carve-out exempting trade Proclamations from the normal OMB-clearance requirement otherwise imposed by EO 11030, and Sec. 4(f)'s explicit non-disclosure restriction on International Trade Commission advice. Also establishes the East-West Foreign Trade Board, directly implementing the historically significant Jackson-Vanik-adjacent freedom-of-emigration trade linkage (Trade Act Secs. 402/409) between US-Soviet trade relations and Jewish emigration rights. Diff-aware: cleanly abolishes EO 11075 (this stream, 0.0%) and EO 11789 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 13, 6.25%).

**FACA's 2-year sunset requirement validated at real scale.** EO 11827 (Continuance of Certain Federal Advisory Committees, 16.67%) is a routine biennial renewal exercise, but its own text confirms that more than 300 federal advisory committees have already been abolished or allowed to lapse under FACA's built-in sunset since the Act's 1973 effective date — a second, much larger-scale confirmation (after EO 11737's Souris-Red-Rainy finding, this stream, Batch 4 chunk 11) that this stream's tracked sunset-clause convention corresponds to a mechanism that genuinely functions as intended, not just on paper.

**The energy-office restructuring saga reaches its most historically consequential link yet.** EO 11834 (Activation of ERDA and NRC, 12.5%) is structurally narrow — just an effective-date-setting and administrative-transfer order — but activates the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (still the primary US nuclear-safety regulator, 50+ years later) and ERDA (direct ancestor of the 1977 Department of Energy), splitting the old Atomic Energy Commission. Sixth link in this stream's tracked energy-office sequence (EO 11712 -> EO 11726 -> EO 11748 -> EO 11775, all Nixon -> EO 11814 -> this order, Ford).

**An early data point in a debate that resurfaces much later in this stream.** EO 11839 (Regional-office civil-service positions converted to noncareer/Schedule C, 18.75%) converts senior regional-official career-service positions across six departments/agencies to at-will political appointments — flagged now for cross-referencing against a far more sweeping instance of this same underlying civil-service-politicization pattern many decades later in this stream ("Schedule F"). Meaningfully offset by an explicit incumbent-protection savings clause.

**The Clemency Board's application window needed extending twice.** EO 11837 and EO 11842 (both 0.0%) push the Presidential Clemency Board's (EO 11803, this stream, Batch 4 chunk 1 [Ford], 12.5%) application deadline from January 31 to March 1 to March 31, 1975 — three total deadlines, suggesting genuine difficulty getting eligible applicants to come forward within the originally-planned window.

**A well-checked statutory exemption mechanism.** EO 11840 (ARBA waiver authority, 11.11%) creates an explicit exemption from standard procurement/travel-authority limitations for the Bicentennial-era ARBA, meaningfully checked by a mandatory, documented per-use written-justification requirement (Sec. 2) — a positive design worth remembering as a comparison point.

**Routine templates and positive-design continuations:** the EO 10973 hub-order-echo pattern gets a second touch in this same batch (EO 11841, 12.5%, adding a new Development Coordination Committee, following EO 11816, this stream, Batch 4 chunk 1 [Ford], 6.25%); the balanced-tripartite-representation pattern from EO 11809 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 1 [Ford]) extends directly into the construction sector (EO 11849, 6.25%); a comprehensive, mostly-refining amendment to EO 11491 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 2, 33.33%) scores meaningfully lower than its parent (EO 11838, 12.5%); a genuinely due-process-strengthening Manual for Courts-Martial revision (EO 11835, 6.25%, continuing EO 11476, this stream, Batch 4 chunk 1); a clean, risk-reducing gold-regulation deregulation following Congress's 1974 restoration of private gold ownership (EO 11825, 0.0%); a landmark, largely self-restrictive chemical-herbicide/riot-control-agent renunciation issued in the direct aftermath of the Agent Orange controversy (EO 11850, 18.75%).

### Chunk 3 (EO 11851-11877, 25 EOs) — complete: the founding of CFIUS, and the Fall of Saigon

**The founding order of CFIUS — flagged prominently for essential future cross-referencing.** EO 11858 (Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States, 18.75%), issued May 7, 1975, is the founding order of what would become one of the most consequential national-security review bodies in the federal government. At this 1975 founding stage the Committee has purely monitoring/coordinating functions and no transaction-blocking power — that would not arrive until the 1988 Exon-Florio Amendment, and CFIUS's jurisdiction and mandatory-filing requirements would later be dramatically expanded by the 2018 FIRRMA reforms (which will fall within this stream's Trump-1st-term batch). One genuine transparency finding (Sec. 5's confidentiality provision on submitted investment data) offset by an explicit Construction clause (Sec. 6) preserving other agencies' authority.

**A direct governmental response to the Fall of Saigon.** EO 11860 (President's Advisory Committee on Refugees, 12.5%), established May 19, 1975 — less than three weeks after the Fall of Saigon (April 30, 1975) — coordinated the resettlement of what would become over 100,000 Indochinese refugees. A genuine, clean one-year sunset (Sec. 2) offset by one modest vague-standard finding (Sec. 3(a)'s broad "funds... as the Committee may require" language).

**The first Jackson-Vanik-adjacent waiver in this stream.** EO 11854 (Trade Act Sec. 402 waiver for Romania, 16.67%) directly implements EO 11846's (this stream, Batch 5 chunk 2, 31.25%) freshly-established East-West Foreign Trade Board framework, tied to the historic US-Soviet-bloc trade/emigration linkage debate. ### Chunk 4 (EO 11878-11904, 25 EOs) — complete: the Sinai peace mission, and a re-centralization within a single administration

**A historically landmark Middle East peace-process mechanism, and this chunk's highest score.** EO 11896 (United States Sinai Support Mission, 25.0%) implements the "Early Warning System in Sinai" under the September 1975 Egyptian-Israeli Sinai II Disengagement Agreement -- a direct US institutional precursor to the Multinational Force and Observers (MFO), the peacekeeping mechanism that would formalize after the 1979 Camp David-era peace treaty. Two genuine, real findings: Sec. 3(a)(5)'s contractor-indemnification waiver directly echoes the moral-hazard logic already flagged at EO 11610 (this stream, 27.27%, Nixon-era defense-contractor indemnification), and Sec. 3 creates NINE separate statutory procurement exemptions -- broader than EO 11840's ARBA precedent (this stream, Batch 5 chunk 2, 11.11%) -- though meaningfully checked by the same mandatory written per-use justification requirement.

**A genuine re-centralization, reversing the same administration's own prior decentralization.** EO 11893 (Transferring GSA policy functions to OMB, 18.75%) reclaims policy-formulation authority that EO 11717 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 10, 7.14% -- the order that launched this stream's tracked "EOP-decentralization counter-narrative") had moved OUT of OMB in 1973, and even reverses part of Ford's OWN six-month-old EO 11867 (this stream, Batch 5 chunk 3, 6.25%). **Two more touches to the Federal Regional Council saga.** EO 11892 (12.5%) is the latest link in this stream's longest-tracked EOP-centralization thread (EO 11541 -> EO 11647, this stream, Batch 4 chunk 8, 31.25% -> EO 11731, this stream, Batch 4 chunk 11, 31.25% -> this order), adding Commerce and the Federal Energy Administration to the Council system while keeping the OMB Deputy Director as Chairman.

**A foundational nuclear-nonproliferation coordination mechanism, notable for a genuinely well-designed escalation clause.** EO 11902 (Nuclear export licensing procedures, 12.5%) establishes the Executive Branch's interagency framework for informing NRC nuclear export licensing decisions -- a direct institutional predecessor to the modern nuclear export-control regime. **A foundational federal wage-garnishment framework, still operative decades later.** EO 11881 (12.5%) establishes the uniform CSC-administered framework implementing Social Security Act Sec. 459 (added by the Social Services Amendments of 1974) for garnishing federal employees' pay for child support/alimony obligations, with a genuine mandatory Federal Register publication requirement (Sec. 4).

### Chunk 5 (EO 11905-11931, 25 EOs) — complete: the landmark intelligence-community reform, and the energy-office saga's culmination

**The most historically significant single order in this entire stream to date.** EO 11905 (United States Foreign Intelligence Activities, 33.33%) is Ford's post-Watergate/Church-Committee/Rockefeller-Commission (EO 11828, this stream, Batch 5 chunk 3, 18.75%) intelligence-community reform — the direct structural ancestor of Carter's EO 12036 (1978) and Reagan's EO 12333 (1981), the latter of which remains the executive branch's governing intelligence framework decades later. Home to the historic, unconditional assassination ban (Sec. 5(g): "No employee of the United States Government shall engage in, or conspire to engage in, political assassination") and the first-of-its-kind Intelligence Oversight Board (Sec. 6). Evaluated strictly on structural grounds, the order is a genuine mix: real, substantial accountability ADDITIONS (the Oversight Board, the enumerated Sec. 5 domestic-surveillance restrictions) sit alongside a genuine structural gap — Sec. 5(b)(2)'s domestic electronic-surveillance authorization requires only Attorney-General-approved procedures, with no judicial warrant requirement anywhere in the order, a full two years before FISA (1978) would close exactly this gap. CRITICAL on Flag 9 given its extraordinary, enduring institutional legacy.

**The energy-office restructuring saga reaches at least its seventh major touch, spanning two administrations.** EO 11930 (Federal Energy Office within EOP, 31.25%, chunk high) concentrates FEA's practical operations — inherited from EO 11790 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 13, 38.89% CRITICAL) and EO 11912 (this same chunk, 18.75%) — in a single EOP-housed Administrator with no described independent tenure protection. Notably reuses the exact same name, "Federal Energy Office," as EO 11748's original December 1973 crisis-response body (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 12, 37.5% CRITICAL) — a potentially confusing institutional-naming echo three years later. Positive design feature: Sec. 10's mandatory five-day EPA-comment period before environmentally-significant rules, with a bounded 14-day emergency-waiver window. EO 11912 (EPCA delegation, 18.75%, same chunk) is the direct implementing order for the Energy Policy and Conservation Act of 1975 — still the legal basis for the Strategic Petroleum Reserve and CAFE fuel-economy standards decades later — and explicitly cross-references EO 11790 to avoid jurisdictional ambiguity (Flag 10 ABSENT).

**A clean within-chunk contrast pair on presidential-reservation design, continuing last chunk's theme.** EO 11910 (18.75%) amends EO 11795 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 13, 6.25%) and REMOVES the President's personal reservation of disaster-relief legal-services authority. Both concern emergency/disaster-adjacent assistance funds; the administration reaches opposite structural design choices within weeks of each other — a clean demonstration of this stream's diff-aware methodology catching both a regression and a preservation in the same policy family.

**A landmark disability-rights order, direct ancestor of the ADA.** EO 11914 (Nondiscrimination With Respect to the Handicapped, 18.75%) implements Rehabilitation Act Sec. 504 government-wide, continuing the thread first tracked at EO 11758 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 12, 22.22%, Sec. 503 contractor delegation). **A genuinely well-designed transparency order.** EO 11920 (CAB Presidential-review procedures, 6.25%) requires a mandatory public docket for written ex parte-adjacent communications (Sec. 5(a)) and bars EOP staff from discussing pending cases with private parties absent a documented exception (Sec. 4) — matching the positive ethics-design tradition of EO 11570 (this stream, Batch 4 chunk 5, 6.25%).

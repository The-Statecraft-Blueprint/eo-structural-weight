### Chunk 4 (EO 12753-12778 excl. gap 12760, 25 EOs) — complete: Civil Justice Reform's self-insulating design, a clean wartime sunset paired with a new Haiti emergency, and the Federal Salary Council joins the modern locality-pay system

**This chunk's clearest CRITICAL-flag order, and the most structurally significant order in the batch to date.** EO12778 (Civil Justice Reform, 55.56%) is a landmark, historically influential litigation-reform order - explicitly framed in its own preamble as intended to 'provide a model for similar reforms of litigation practices in the private sector and in various states,' and echoed in the 1993 Federal Rules of Civil Procedure amendments. ### Chunk 6 (EO 12805-12833 excl. gaps, 26 EOs, batch-closing) — complete: the Haitian interdiction program's 'unreviewable discretion,' a still-operative industrial security program, and the final Bush EO

**This batch's highest-scoring and most consequential order.** EO12807 (Interdiction of Illegal Aliens, 70.0%) authorized the Coast Guard to interdict Haitian migrant vessels on the high seas and return them without a mandated refugee-status-determination procedure, vesting the Attorney General with sole 'unreviewable discretion' (Sec.2(c)(3)) to grant an exception - about as textually blunt an accountability-elimination structure as this stream has documented. This exact program was upheld by the Supreme Court in Sale v. Haitian Centers Council (1993), and it operated in direct tandem with this batch's Haiti national-emergency orders (EO12775/EO12779, this stream, this batch).

**A second still-operative, foundational order.** EO12829 (National Industrial Security Program, 50.0%) remains the legal foundation of the U.S. contractor security-clearance system today (administered by DCSA). **The Bosnian war enters the corpus, with a rare same-administration loophole closure.** EO12808 (33.33%) declares a new emergency blocking Serbian/Montenegrin government property; EO12810 (50.0% CRITICAL) escalates it to a comprehensive UN-mandated embargo six days later - the same initial-block-then-comprehensive-embargo pattern seen at Kuwait and Haiti - with an unusually broad category list including sporting-event and cultural-exchange prohibitions.

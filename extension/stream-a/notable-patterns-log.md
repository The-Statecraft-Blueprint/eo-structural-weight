# Stream A Extension: Notable Patterns Log

## Batch 11 COMPLETE (337/337): the Steel Seizure crisis (EO10340, struck down in *Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer*) and EO10290's founding classification system tie the all-time-high 75% and 62.5%, bracketing the McCarthy-era loyalty apparatus's furthest reach into the United Nations

Batch 11 (Truman 1950-1953) finished at 337/337 (322 coded this stream). This batch covers the entire Korean War, the second Red Scare's peak, and Truman's final eighteen months in office, and it produced the single most constitutionally consequential document in the whole stream. **EO10340** (April 8, 1952, "Directing the Secretary of Commerce to Take Possession of and Operate the Plants and Facilities of Certain Steel Companies") — the Steel Seizure order — scores 62.5% with CRITICAL on Flags 1, 7, and 9: unlike every prior seizure order in this stream (all grounded in a specific statute — the Selective Service Act, the Defense Production Act, war-powers legislation), this order rests solely on "the authority vested in me by the Constitution and laws of the United States," identifying no statute at all. Seven weeks later the Supreme Court struck it down 6-3 in *Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer*, and Justice Jackson's concurrence — the three-zone framework for evaluating presidential power — remains the most-cited authority in American separation-of-powers law to this day. Its direct institutional aftermath, **EO10377** (reconstituting the Wage Stabilization Board with full Senate confirmation after the Board's labor/industry members walked out during the steel dispute), was coded CRITICAL on Flag 9 as a rare case of a crisis producing genuine accountability-strengthening rather than erosion. Separately, **EO10290** (Sept. 24, 1951, the first comprehensive government-wide security-classification system — Top Secret/Secret/Confidential/Restricted) ties the stream's all-time-high **75%** score (previously held by EO9412/9489/10155), with CRITICAL on Flags 4, 7, and 9: it is the direct institutional ancestor of the modern classification regime (EO10501 through EO13526), built on self-referential, agency-defined classification standards with no external check — a structure that would produce the Pentagon Papers controversy two decades later. The batch's other major thread is the loyalty-security apparatus's furthest extension: **EO10241** (lowering the loyalty standard from "reasonable grounds for belief of disloyalty" to mere "reasonable doubt as to loyalty") was applied to Public Health Service officers at EO10280, and then — most strikingly — to American citizens employed at the **United Nations Secretariat** at **EO10422** (Jan. 9, 1953, 50%, CRITICAL on Flag 9), which had the U.S. government investigate its own citizens working for a nominally independent international body and transmit loyalty determinations to the UN Secretary-General, a program that drew contemporary criticism for compromising UN personnel independence. Other significant finds: **EO10421** (Physical Security of Facilities Important to National Defense, 58.3%, CRITICAL on Flags 3 and 9) establishes an early critical-infrastructure-protection apparatus spanning private industry — a direct conceptual ancestor of the post-9/11 CISA/NIPP framework; **EO10392** (President's Commission on Immigration and Naturalization) was established weeks after Congress overrode Truman's veto of the McCarran-Walter Act, and its "Whom We Shall Welcome" report laid groundwork for the 1965 Immigration and Nationality Act; **EO10250** delegates the President's authority to remove and replace tribal chiefs of the Five Civilized Tribes, a still-startling instance of direct federal control over Native self-governance inherited from 1906; **EO10240** implements the sex-discriminatory pregnancy/parenthood discharge policy for women service members that would persist for two decades; **EO10263** implements the landmark 1950 Panama Canal reorganization; **EO10264/10265/10408** track the Pacific-territories civilianization thread (Samoa, the Trust Territory, and a partial reversal at Tinian/Saipan back to Navy control); and **EO10281** establishes the Defense Materials Procurement Agency, extending WWII-era RFC/Export-Import Bank industrial-financing mechanisms to the Korean War mobilization. A minor recurring pattern: the PHS-as-military-service declaration (EO10349) was extended four times in two-week increments (EO10356/10362/10367) as Congress repeatedly passed short-term extensions of the underlying Emergency Powers Interim Continuation Act, a clear case of legislative-executive "patchwork" emergency-authority renewal distinct from this stream's broader "zombie emergency" pattern. This closes out the FDR/Truman portion of Stream A (Batches 1-11, 1936-1953) in its entirety.

## Batch 10 progress (180/277): EO 9957 (1948 nationwide rail seizure, 71.4%) echoes EO 9727, and the Selective Service Act of 1948 machinery (EO 9979/9988/9992/10001) rebuilds the peacetime draft

Batch 10 (Truman 1947-1949) progress: 180/277 after four chunks (EO 9818-10003). Two major threads dominate this stretch. First, **EO 9957** (May 10, 1948, "Possession, Control, and Operation of Certain Railroads") is the seizure order for the 1948 nationwide rail labor crisis, diff-aware repeating EO 9727's (prior batch, 72.2%, second-highest score in the stream) "standing"/unbounded-class seizure design almost verbatim — the Secretary of the Army may seize "any OTHER carrier by railroad located in the continental United States" without further findings — combined with an eighth appearance of the recurring judicial/creditor-remedy-bar clause (Section 8) and Secretary-controlled (rather than fixed-date) termination. It lands at 71.4%, confirming that this "standing seizure authority" architecture was not a one-off 1946 escalation but a durable toolkit feature reused for the next major rail crisis two years later. Second, this stretch contains the actual re-establishment of the peacetime military draft under the Selective Service Act of 1948 (signed in response to the Berlin Blockade), coded across four separate but interlocking orders: **EO 9979** (July 20, registration/administrative framework, 37.5%) and **EO 9988** (Aug. 20, classification rules and appeals, 42.9%) together build the local-board/appeal-board machinery — notably a genuine model of disclosed due process (written notice, personal-appearance rights, conscientious-objector referral to DOJ, an appeal board expressly "independent of the Director of Selective Service") tempered by the Director's broad, largely unbounded §622.23 override power to reclassify "without regard to eligibility." **EO 9992** (Aug. 28, alien-exemption refinements) and **EO 10001** (Sept. 17, the actual induction-requisition directive, 41.7%) complete the picture — EO 10001 is the order that operationalizes actual delivery of specific individuals for military service, the machinery that would be repeatedly invoked through the Korean War, the Cold War buildup, and Vietnam until the draft's 1973 suspension. Also notable: **EO 9980** (Fair Employment Practices Within the Federal Establishment, July 26, 1948) is Truman's landmark federal-workforce anti-discrimination order, issued the same day as the companion EO 9981 desegregating the armed forces — a rare case where high historical/civil-rights significance coincides with a genuinely protective, multi-layered accountability design (Fair Employment Officer -> department head appeal -> CSC Board -> President), scoring only 25% because the structure itself is rights-expanding rather than power-concentrating. Five Taft-Hartley §206 Boards of Inquiry appear this stretch (EO 9959 communications, EO 9964/9987 maritime, EO 9970 coal, matching the established 25% template with the recurring open-ended "until terminated by the President" continuation clause), alongside a continuing run of clean Railway Labor Act emergency boards. EO 9943 ("Providing for Carrying Out the Foreign Assistance Act of 1948") is the actual Marshall Plan implementing order, CRITICAL on Flags 7 and 9 (50%) for its disclosed procurement-safeguard exemptions enabling emergency-speed European Recovery Program disbursements exceeding $13 billion.

## NEW SECOND-HIGHEST SCORE: EO 9727 (nationwide 1946 railroad strike seizure, 72.2%), plus three institutional reversals of the stabilization apparatus in six months

Batch 9 progress: 215/280 after five chunks (EO 9629-9772, spanning late 1945 into August 1946). The standout find is **EO 9727** (May 17, 1946, "Possession, Control, and Operation of Certain Railroads") — the seizure order for the major nationwide 1946 railroad strike crisis, which within days led Truman to ask Congress for standby authority to draft striking railroad workers into the Army. It names over 330 carriers (effectively the entire U.S. rail system) AND grants the ODT Director explicit "standing" authority to seize "any OTHER carrier by railroad located in the continental United States" without further findings — the unbounded-class design first identified in EO 9536 (prior batch), now applied at unprecedented national scale, combined with the recurring judicial/creditor-remedy-bar clause (its seventh appearance in this stream: EO 9412, 9462, 9554, 9602, 9658, 9693, now 9727). It lands at **72.2%, the second-highest score in the entire stream**, behind only the 75% tie between EO 9412 and EO 9489. The postwar wage-price stabilization apparatus also underwent remarkable institutional churn in just six months: **EO 9620** (Sept. 1945, prior batch) concentrated stabilization authority in a single OWMR-designated Administrator; **EO 9699** (Feb. 21, 1946) reversed course, restoring a broad multi-stakeholder Economic Stabilization Board (multiple Cabinet secretaries plus two representatives each of labor, management, and farmers); and **EO 9762** (July 25, 1946) reversed course again, re-concentrating authority under OWMR and demoting the Board to a purely advisory role — CRITICAL on Flag 9 as the clearest illustration in this stream of an institution's structural instability directly tracking its approach to political collapse (OPA's price-control authority would lapse and be fought over in Congress through mid-1946). Two additional bookend terminations closed out major wartime chapters: **EO 9631** (terminating the Office of Censorship, whose founding order EO 8985 remains one of the highest-scoring orders in the entire stream at 70%) and **EO 9742** (terminating and liquidating the War Relocation Authority, the agency that administered Japanese American internment under EO 9102) — both coded as clean, narrow wind-downs structurally, but flagged CRITICAL on Flag 9 given the exceptional severity of the institutions they closed. **EO 9645** (terminating EO 9489's Hawaii military-exclusion authority, which had tied the stream's 75% record) was coded with particular care: it explicitly preserves the validity of all convictions and penalties imposed under the terminated authority, with no retroactive review mechanism created — a genuine, disclosed limitation flagged under Flag 7 even though the order's overall direction is restorative. Other finds: **EO 9633/9634** jointly implement the historically foundational "Truman Proclamation" on the Continental Shelf and coastal fisheries (CRITICAL Flag 9, direct ancestor of the modern Law of the Sea framework); **EO 9630** dissolves the Foreign Economic Administration (CRITICAL-scored in the prior batch at 68.75%) across four separate destination agencies; and **EO 9735** establishes the Cabinet Committee on Palestine that translated the Anglo-American Committee of Inquiry's findings into policy, a pivotal link toward the 1947 UN Partition Plan. ## Batch 7 begins: EO 9024 (War Production Board) — first quadruple-CRITICAL order, 68.75%

As anticipated when logging the EO 8839→8875→8900 arc at the close of Batch 6, the War Production Board (EO 9024, Jan. 16, 1942) is the culminating institutional event of that pre-war economic-mobilization buildup. It abolishes the already-CRITICAL Supply Priorities and Allocations Board outright (transferring its personnel, records, and property), asserts unilateral authority to "direct such changes in [OPM's] organization as he may deem necessary," and contains the single most extreme preemption-of-review clause found in this corpus to date: "his decisions shall be final." ## NEW RECORD HIGH: EO 8734, Office of Price Administration and Civilian Supply (56.25%) — surpasses the freezing-order/CPS peak

EO 8734 (April 11, 1941) creates OPACS, the direct institutional precursor to the wartime Office of Price Administration — the agency that would go on to run comprehensive nationwide price controls, rationing, and rent stabilization for the duration of WWII. This is the first order in the entire stream to fire EIGHT applicable flags, including CRITICAL on Flag 9 and the first-ever combination of Flags 1, 2, 3, 4 (Power Concentration, Accountability Gaps, Bundling, Vague Enforcement) ALL firing together: a single Administrator is vested with self-judged authority to set "fair and reasonable" prices economy-wide, a self-judged sub-delegation of Presidential authority under multiple additional statutes, no disclosed hearing/appeal process, and a bundled portfolio spanning price-setting, materials allocation, anti-hoarding enforcement, and rent-stabilization program development. Flag 10 (Inter-Agency Cannibalization) also fires, given OPACS's explicit reach into other departments' procurement/pricing decisions and its advisory position over the Office of Production Management. Final score 56.25% — a new record high, surpassing the freezing-order family and EO 8675 (CPS program) at 50%. Notably, this order predates the actual statutory Emergency Price Control Act (Jan 1942) by nine months, meaning its price-setting authority at this stage is not yet backed by compulsory legal enforcement — a genuine mitigating fact that was weighed but did not change the CRITICAL Flag 9 call, given the institution's eventual scale and consequence. Worth flagging for cross-reference when Batch 7-8 (1942+) reach the EPCA itself and OPA's subsequent build-out.

Also this stretch: EO 8716 (National Defense Mediation Board, March 19, 1941) — the NDMB is the institutional precursor to the compulsory 1942 War Labor Board, but scored a modest 16.67%, reflecting its genuinely balanced tripartite structure (11 members, 3 public/4 labor/4 employer), voluntary-arbitration-only design (binding only with both parties' consent), and Secretary of Labor gatekeeping — a useful contrast case showing that "precursor to a major wartime institution" does not automatically mean a high score; the actual disclosed structural safeguards matter more than the institution's eventual historical importance. ## EO 8781: universal federal-employee fingerprinting — a genuinely unbounded surveillance-infrastructure order (40%)

EO 8781 (June 12, 1941) requires fingerprinting of essentially the entire executive civil service workforce, with records transmitted to the FBI for "permanent classification and filing." ## EO 8807: Office of Scientific Research and Development (35.71%) — CRITICAL Flag 9 despite genuinely robust internal checks

EO 8807 (June 28, 1941) creates OSRD under Vannevar Bush — the institution that would go on to coordinate radar development, the proximity fuze, penicillin mass production, and, through its S-1 Section, the direct scientific precursor to the Manhattan Project. ## NEW RECORD HIGH: EO 8839, Economic Defense Board (64.29%) — first CRITICAL rating on Flag 10 (Inter-Agency Cannibalization)

EO 8839 (July 30, 1941) establishes the Economic Defense Board, chaired by the Vice President, with Cabinet-level membership spanning State, Treasury, War, Justice, Navy, Agriculture, and Commerce. It surpasses OPACS as the stream's new record high, driven by two CRITICAL flags: Flag 9 (the Board, renamed the Board of Economic Warfare that December, became central to Allied economic-warfare strategy and significant internal wartime policy disputes) and — for the first time in the entire stream — Flag 10 (Inter-Agency Cannibalization) CRITICAL. Section 4 contains explicit, binding subordination language unlike anything found so far: "the administration of the various activities relating to economic defense shall remain with the several departments and agencies now charged with such duties but such administration shall conform to the policies formulated or approved by the Board" — naming at least 25 departments and agencies (including OPACS, OPM, and the Export Control Administrator) required to conform to the Board's policy determinations. Combined with a Chairman's unilateral final-decision-making authority (Section 7, "to make final decisions when necessary to expedite the work of the Board"), this is a genuinely different order of magnitude from prior "coordinating" institutions like EO 8632 or EO 8757, which only inferentially raised turf-friction concerns. This is a textually explicit hierarchical-subordination clause, the clearest Flag 10 evidence in the stream to date.

Same-day companion order EO 8840 (Office of the Coordinator of Inter-American Affairs, Nelson Rockefeller, 35.71%) makes a useful contrast: structurally similar broad-contracting-authority design to OSRD, but explicitly COOPERATIVE rather than subordinating language toward other agencies (Flag 10 ABSENT), and softer cultural/commercial-diplomacy consequence (Flag 9 PRESENT not CRITICAL) — reinforcing that textual subordination language, not merely institutional prominence, is what should drive Flag 10 scoring.

## EO 8832: freezing-order extension to Japan (50%) — the single most consequential instance of the family

EO 8832 (July 26, 1941) extends the by-now-familiar freezing-order mechanism to China and Japan, issued in direct response to Japan's occupation of southern French Indochina. ## Coding convention: tract-specific Taylor Grazing Act land designations and Flag 6

A large share of FDR 1936 orders are narrow amendments to EO 6910 (the blanket 1934 Taylor Grazing Act withdrawal) or free-standing revocations of much older individual land withdrawals pending resurvey. Adopted convention, logged here for auditability rather than left implicit in each record:

- **Pure revocations pending resurvey** (EO 7264, 7269, 7271, 7272, 7275, and structurally the Sec. 1 half of EO 7270): scored Flag 6 **NOT_APPLICABLE** — a one-time, self-executing reversal creates no new ongoing authority to sunset.
- **Tract-specific carve-outs granting a new bounded, defined use** (EO 7266 stock driveway, EO 7267 air-navigation facilities, EO 7270's Sec. 2 lookout stations): scored Flag 6 **ABSENT** under the "standing procedural framework" carve-out (v1.2.1) — administrative/operational land dedication, no natural end-state tied to an emergency or crisis, and this exact corpus shows the category is actively revised through ordinary supersession (the revocation-template orders above are direct evidence of that within the same batch).
- **EO 7265** is the strongest version of this: it has its own explicit textual termination trigger (60-day vacate-on-notice), not just inherited pattern-evidence from sibling orders — used that instead of leaning on the general convention.
- **EO 7270** is flagged explicitly as a judgment call: it's the first order in the batch to state the "continues... unless and until revoked" boilerplate directly. Scored on the same substantive basis as the others (no natural end-state) rather than treating the presence of that boilerplate phrase as automatically dispositive. Worth revisiting if a reviewer disagrees with treating boilerplate presence/absence as non-dispositive.

## Confirmed instance of the civil-service-bypass pattern from Known Limitations (EO 7276)

## A new pattern candidate: chronic reorganization postponement (EO 7390)

## Batch 03 — Johnson (in progress)

**EO 11130, the Warren Commission's founding order, has two genuine structural gaps relative to this stream's other ad hoc commissions.** No reporting deadline is specified anywhere in the order's own text (contrast EO 11100 and EO 11144, both this chunk, which each set explicit deadlines), and no self-termination clause appears at all — an actual absence, not an omission that's typical for this stream's commission template. Membership is deliberately diffuse across all three branches (Chief Justice, two Senators, two Representatives, two former executive officials) rather than concentrated in a single office. Flag 9 scored CRITICAL solely for the order's enduring historical significance, evaluated strictly as a structural/governance matter without assessing the Commission's substantive conclusions or the public debate surrounding them.

**Fifth confirmed instance of the named-individual civil-service-exemption template: EO 11138 (Frederick Strauss, Jan. 7 1964), the first such instance under the Johnson administration.** Following EO 10778 (Batch 1), EO 10959 and EO 11024 (Batch 2), this continues unbroken across the administration transition — notably the weakest-justified instance found so far, offering no stated rationale at all (not even the general "public interest" boilerplate used in earlier instances).

**EO 11007 (this stream's Batch 2 chunk 4 model advisory-committee order) is being actively reused as a governance template, not treated as a one-off.** Both EO 11136 (Consumer Advisory Council) and EO 11143 (Public Advisory Committee for Trade Negotiations), both this chunk, explicitly incorporate EO 11007's mandatory meeting-record/disclosure/review provisions into their own operation rather than creating freestanding, unreviewed bodies — confirming the positive design identified in Batch 2 was becoming standard administration practice, not an isolated instance.

**Positive design note: EO 11144 (Temporary Alaska Claims Commission, Mar. 5 1964) requires Senate confirmation of its members** — an unusually strong accountability feature compared to this stream's typical presidentially-appointed-only ad hoc commissions — paired with a genuine, specifically-defined 60-day self-termination clause.

**Recurring non-encroachment clause pattern continues into the Johnson administration: EO 11150 (Federal Reconstruction and Development Planning Commission for Alaska, Apr. 2 1964).** Its Sec. 5 non-encroachment clause is nearly identical to EO 11122's (this stream's Batch 2 chunk 8, Rural Development Committee), confirming this protective boilerplate continued to be used consistently for major new coordinating commissions across the administration transition, here in response to the historically significant 1964 Alaska earthquake.

**New template identified: DoD/civilian-agency wartime-contingency subordination (EO 11161, DoD-FAA relations, July 7 1964, scored 40% — the highest score of chunk 2 and second-highest of the batch so far).** Directs DoD and the FAA to develop advance plans for the FAA's contemplated wartime transfer under Sec. 302(e) of the Federal Aviation Act. The order's entire operative limit on how much of the FAA the Secretary of Defense may absorb is the undefined standard "to the extent deemed by the Secretary to be necessary," repeated three times, with no objective ceiling. It also extends the arrangement's practical reach to "any period of national emergency short of war," beyond the statute's own declared-war trigger. Flagged CRITICAL on both Vague Enforcement (Flag 4) and Inter-Agency Cannibalization (Flag 10) — the only double-CRITICAL order found in this batch so far. Worth watching for recurrence: similar civilian-agency-into-DoD contingency templates may reappear in later Cold War-era chunks.

**9th confirmed touch on the EO 10530 hub order: EO 11164 (Aug. 1 1964).** Updates the Alaska Omnibus Act property-transfer delegation and explicitly preserves the existing carve-out to EO 10857's separate Interior Department delegation — mechanism preserved, not weakened or strengthened, consistent with the hub order's pattern of narrow corrective touches.

**Clean diff-aware "mechanism preserved unchanged across the administration transition" finding: EO 11179 (National Defense Executive Reserve, Sept. 22 1964) vs. its predecessor EO 10660 (Batch 1, Eisenhower, Feb. 15 1956).** Verified directly against EO 10660's flag-by-flag coding in the DB: the 3-year individual-service cap, training-only limitation, and annual report-to-the-President requirement all carry forward unchanged, with only the administering office's name updated (Office of Defense Mobilization → Office of Emergency Planning). Both orders land at an identical 8.33%. This also continues the ODM/OEP-Director authority-accumulation narrative first tracked in Batch 1.

**Canal Zone Merit System (EO 11171, Aug. 18 1964) is this chunk's most substantive standing-governance order (20%).** Establishes a full parallel personnel system with a genuine exemptions-architecture core (Sec. 2(a)(1) power to exclude any employee/position from coverage), but notably pairs it with real checks: CSC concurrence for interchange, a five-member Board of Appeals, CSC periodic review, and a mandatory multi-agency consultation requirement before rulemaking — one of the better-checked exemption structures found in this stream.

**Positive counter-example for Sunset Provisions: EO 11177 (Columbia River Treaty arrangements, Sept. 16 1964).** Sec. 301 contains an explicit, affirmative reservation: "There is hereby reserved the right to modify or terminate any or all of the provisions of this order" — a genuine self-termination clause, unlike the silence typical of most orders in this stream. Also pairs with an explicit anti-cannibalization clause preserving existing Army/Interior departmental responsibilities.

**Three RLA Emergency Board template confirmations this chunk alone (EO 11168, 11169, 11170, all Aug. 18 1964, plus EO 11180, Sept. 24) — all scored 0.0% as expected**, continuing the self-terminating 30-day-report/30-day-status-quo pattern tracked since Batch 1/2. One LMRA/Taft-Hartley Board of Inquiry (EO 11181, Sept. 30) confirmed the separate indefinite-continuation template (Flag 6 PRESENT) — the two templates remain cleanly distinguishable.

**EO 11157 (incentive/sea/subsistence/quarters pay consolidation, June 22 1964) revokes 13 prior orders in one stroke, including two from within this stream's own corpus: EO 11120 (Batch 2, 0.0%) and EO 11146 (Batch 3 chunk 1, 0.0%).** Since both revoked in-stream predecessors were already minimal-risk, this consolidation represents no strengthening or weakening of prior mechanism — just administrative unification. The order also repeats an explicit inter-service-uniformity clause ("uniform for all the services to the fullest extent practicable") across all four of its Parts, an anti-cannibalization design consistent with the Emergency Preparedness Functions series template from Batch 2.

**Highest-significance diff-aware finding of the batch so far: EO 11205 (Mar. 15 1965) abolishes, in a single sentence, the office and authority created by EO 10729 (this stream, Batch 1/Eisenhower, 1957, scored 60.0% with FOUR separate CRITICAL flags — Power Concentration, Vague Enforcement, Preemption of Oversight, Exemptions Architecture).** EO 10729 had given an unconfirmed White House "Special Assistant to the President for Personnel Management" unilateral, criteria-free power to exempt any Civil Service Commission matter from presidential-approval consultation, and to revoke that exemption, entirely at his own discretion. Eight years later this order eliminates that office outright, with no replacement structure. A clean example of a previously CRITICAL-flagged structural risk actually being resolved rather than perpetuated.

**EO 11188 (Amending the Selective Service Regulations, Nov. 17 1964) is this stream's most structurally significant exemptions-architecture order to date (Flag 11 CRITICAL, overall 25%).** A comprehensive rewrite of draft registration exemptions and classification categories (diplomatic personnel, exchange visitors/students, National Guard/Reserve members, sole surviving sons, physically/mentally/morally disqualified registrants) issued as Vietnam-era draft calls were intensifying. Evaluated strictly as a structural/governance matter (breadth and stakes of the exemption system), consistent with the project's neutral treatment of EO 11130 (Warren Commission, chunk 1). Positive design note: the newly-drafted I-Y/IV-F standard requires a war or national emergency "declared by the Congress" — a congressional, not unilateral-executive, trigger, avoiding this stream's recurring zombie-emergency-trap concern.

**Three-in-one-chunk confirmation of the Construction/non-encroachment-clause template for major coordinating committees: EO 11182 (Alaska), EO 11185 (Education), EO 11186 (Appalachia), all Oct. 1964, all scoring 5.0%.** By contrast, EO 11197 (President's Council on Equal Opportunity, Feb. 5 1965, VP-chaired, 16.67%) conspicuously lacks this clause and is additionally empowered (Sec. 4(3)) to recommend "merger, combination, or elimination" of the very civil-rights agencies it coordinates — the first coordinating body this stream has found with an explicit, if only recommendatory, cannibalization-oriented design feature rather than a protective one.

**First observed "hub-order disaggregation" event: EO 11196 (Feb. 2 1965) carves an entire Part (Part III) out of EO 10530 and relocates it, along with related delegations, to a dedicated Housing and Home Finance Administrator order** — and in doing so reverses the Sec. 4(g) addition made by EO 11184 just four months earlier (both this chunk), the fastest reversal of a hub-order touch observed in this stream. This is a distinct sub-pattern from the more common additive-touch pattern (9 prior touches through Batch 2, plus EO 11164 and EO 11184 this batch).

**First observed "exemption clawback" for a specific actor-class: EO 11198 (Feb. 10 1965) explicitly closes EO 11175's (this stream, Batch 3 chunk 2, 20.0%) Canadian-securities tax exemption for commercial-bank acquisitions specifically**, while leaving the exemption fully intact for all other U.S. persons — narrowed for one actor-class, preserved for all others. Lands at an identical 20.0% to its predecessor.

**Fifth confirmed instance of the domestic military-enforcement template: EO 11207 (Mar. 20 1965), issued in direct response to the Selma "Bloody Sunday" events, scores an identical 20.0% to all four prior instances (EO 10730 Little Rock 1957; EO 11053 Mississippi 1962; EO 11111/EO 11118 Alabama 1963).** Genuinely new nuance: this is the first instance where the SecDef's core enforcement authority (Sec. 1) is tied to a concrete end-event ("ending as soon as practicable after the termination of the march") rather than left fully open-ended, though the National Guard federalization clause itself retains the standard indefinite "until relieved by appropriate orders" phrasing.

**Three parallel systemic (non-named-individual) civil-service conversion orders this chunk: EO 11187 (Postal Field Service, one-year cohort window), EO 11202 (Student Trainees, standing/ongoing), EO 11203 (Treasury protective-function employees, standing/ongoing).** All structurally bounded by objective service/examination criteria — a benign, systemic counterpart to this stream's named-individual exemption template (5 confirmed instances through Batch 3 chunk 1). EO 11219 (chunk 4, Foreign Service) confirms this as a genuine recurring template, now 4 instances.

## HUB-ORDER WRAP-UP: EO 10530's disaggregation is now essentially complete (as of chunk 4)

Across Batch 3 chunks 3-4, this stream has watched EO 10530 (this stream's long-tracked omnibus delegation-repository "hub order," base order 0.0%, 9 touches confirmed through Batch 1-2) be systematically dismantled into dedicated, function-specific successor orders over a five-month span in 1965:

- **EO 11196** (Feb. 2 1965, Batch 3 chunk 3, 10.0%): carves out **Part III** (housing functions) → relocated to a dedicated Housing and Home Finance Administrator order, also reversing EO 11184's four-month-old Sec. 4(g) addition.
- **EO 11222** (May 8 1965, this chunk, 22.73%): revokes **Section 2(a)** as part of the new government ethics-standards order.
- **EO 11228** (June 14 1965, this chunk, 15.0%): carves out **Part II** (Civil Service Commission functions) → relocated to a dedicated CSC delegation order.
- **EO 11230** (June 28 1965, this chunk, 10.0%): carves out **Part I** in its entirety → relocated to a dedicated Bureau of the Budget Director order, and explicitly supersedes, **by name**, both EO 11164 (9th touch, 25.0%) and EO 11184 (10th touch, 25.0%) — the two most recent additive touches this stream had been tracking.

By the end of this chunk, all three of EO 10530's major substantive Parts (I, II, III) have been carved out into dedicated successor orders, each anchored to a single accountable office (BOB Director, CSC, HHFA Administrator respectively) rather than remaining scattered across one omnibus repository. This appears to reflect a genuine, deliberate mid-1965 executive-branch reorganization effort, not incidental drift. **Going forward, this stream will track new delegation orders on their own terms rather than continuing to count "touches" against EO 10530, since the hub order's operative content has now been substantially hollowed out.**

**Separately, EO 11222 (Prescribing Standards of Ethical Conduct for Government Officers and Employees, May 8 1965) is one of the most historically significant orders found in this stream to date (22.73%).** It is the direct predecessor of the entire modern federal conflict-of-interest and financial-disclosure regulatory framework. Its most significant structural gap: Sec. 405 requires financial-disclosure statements to be held in **confidence**, disclosed only "for good cause shown" at agency discretion — precisely the feature the post-Watergate Ethics in Government Act of 1978 later reformed by requiring public disclosure. Positive design counterpoint: Sec. 505 carves out agency heads and senior Presidential appointees from the delegated self-waiver authority, preventing senior officials from waiving their own conflicts.

**EO 11223 (Foreign Assistance Act procurement order, May 12 1965) is the highest-scoring order in Batch 3 so far (40.91%, Flag 11 CRITICAL).** It waives, en bloc, at least fourteen distinct federal procurement-integrity statutes for an entire category of foreign-assistance contracting, and grants the Secretary of State open-ended discretion to modify existing contracts "irrespective of rights which may have accrued" whenever he deems it "necessary to protect the foreign policy interests of the United States." The corpus text for this order also contains an apparently mismatched/duplicated country-designation section (see EO 11224 the following day) — flagged transparently as a likely source-document artifact rather than resolved.

**EO 11211 (Japan interest-equalization-tax exemption, Apr. 2 1965) is the first instance in the EO 11175/11198/11211/11224 tax-exemption family to include a hard aggregate dollar cap ($100,000,000/year)** — a genuine quantitative strengthening of the template not present in the Canada (EO 11175) or commercial-bank (EO 11198) versions.

**Naming-vs-substance contrast this chunk: EO 11210 ("Temporary" Commission on Pennsylvania Avenue, Mar. 25 1965) contains no termination clause anywhere in its text**, while EO 11215 (President's Commission on the Patent System, Apr. 8 1965, issued two weeks later) is **not** labeled temporary but contains one of the cleanest, most specific sunset clauses in this stream (18-month final-report deadline + 30-day post-report termination). A useful reminder that an order's title is not a reliable proxy for its actual structural durability.

**EO 11246 (Equal Employment Opportunity, Sept. 24 1965) is one of the most historically consequential orders in this entire stream (27.27%, Flag 9 CRITICAL).** It consolidates federal-contractor EEO enforcement — previously scattered across the multi-agency President's Committee on Equal Employment Opportunity (EO 10925, Batch 2, 40.0%; EO 11114, Batch 2, 15.0%; EO 11162, Batch 3 chunk 2, 0.0%, all superseded and abolished here) — into a single, durable Secretary-of-Labor-led enforcement apparatus (the OFCCP's structural predecessor). Notably strong accountability architecture (Flags 2, 7, 8 all ABSENT: real compliance reporting, hearing rights, Comptroller General notification for debarments) is paired with an open-ended Sec. 204 "national interest" exemption standard (Flag 4) and no sunset (Flag 6, deliberately — this order in fact remained in force for roughly six decades). Evaluated strictly for structural durability and reach, without any assessment of affirmative-action policy's substantive merits.

**Diff-aware closure: EO 11247 (same day, Sept. 24 1965) revokes EO 11197 (this batch, chunk 3, 16.67%)** — the President's Council on Equal Opportunity previously flagged as unusual for lacking the Construction/non-encroachment clause used by its sibling coordinating committees and for its Sec. 4(3) power to recommend merging/eliminating other civil-rights agencies. Seven months after that finding, the Council is abolished outright, its Title VI coordination role relocated to the Attorney General — a narrower, more conventionally accountable office replacing a broader VP-chaired coordinating body. Both EO 11246 and EO 11247, issued the same day, represent a coordinated September 1965 civil-rights-enforcement reorganization.

**EO 11249 (Safeguarding Vessels, Harbors, Ports and Waterfront Facilities, Oct. 10 1965) is a genuinely notable, higher-scoring find (25.0%): vests broad, largely unchecked "security zone" creation and warrantless search/exclusion authority in a single Coast Guard "Captain of the Port,"** bounded only by "for such time as he deems necessary" and "whenever it appears to him that such action is necessary" standards, with no reporting or review requirement. Worth watching for whether later chunks show this authority narrowed or checked.

**New template identified: the "Reorganization Plan No. 4 of 1965 bridge order," confirmed twice in this chunk (EO 11238, National Advisory Council on International Monetary and Financial Problems; EO 11240, Boards of the Foreign Service).** Both re-establish pre-existing statutory bodies with an identical, unusually short and explicit hard termination date: "January 1, 1966, or on such earlier date as may hereafter be prescribed." A clean, deliberately transitional design distinct from this stream's more common indefinite-duration standing bodies.

**EO 11236 (President's Commission on Law Enforcement and Administration of Justice, July 23 1965) — the "Katzenbach Commission" whose 1967 report shaped decades of subsequent U.S. criminal-justice policy — is structurally modest and well-bounded (5.56%)**, with an explicit 18-month report deadline plus 90-day post-report termination, mirroring the design of its DC-focused one-week-earlier sibling EO 11234 (Commission on Crime in the District of Columbia). A reminder that historical significance and structural risk score are independent axes in this methodology.

**New cross-order finding: a single, 16-year-old declared national emergency (Proclamation No. 2914 of December 16, 1950, Korean War-era) still serves as active, unrevisited legal infrastructure for two unrelated 1966 orders in this chunk alone: EO 11270 (Marine Corps lieutenant-general cap suspension) and EO 11281 (blocked-assets transfer from AG to Treasury).** Neither order describes the emergency as lapsed or stale, so neither triggers this stream's zombie-emergency-trap subflag under its strict "acknowledged lapse" definition — but the underlying pattern (a mid-century emergency proclamation quietly continuing to authorize unrelated present-day actions with no periodic re-justification) is real and worth tracking into later batches, especially as Nixon-Ford-Carter-era orders may eventually confront the 1976 National Emergencies Act's formal termination of many such legacy emergencies.

**Confirmed two-stage "Reorganization Plan No. 4 of 1965" implementation template: temporary bridge order, then permanent successor, observed twice in this chunk.** EO 11238 (National Advisory Council on International Monetary and Financial Problems, Batch 3 chunk 5, hard Jan. 1 1966 sunset) is succeeded by EO 11269 (this chunk, permanent, renamed "...Policies"); EO 11240 (Boards of the Foreign Service, Batch 3 chunk 5, same hard sunset) is succeeded by EO 11264 (this chunk, permanent). Both permanent successors explicitly credit/continue the bridge orders' pending business.

**Positive continuity finding: EO 11278 (President's Council and Committee on Recreation and Natural Beauty, May 4 1966) explicitly credits prior Recreation Advisory Council chairmanship service (EO 11017, Batch 2) toward its new rotating-chairmanship term limits (Sec. 101(b))** — meaning the positive rotating-chairmanship design first flagged at EO 11218 (Batch 3 chunk 4, which had just added the TVA Chairman to that rotation less than a year earlier) is not merely preserved through this reorganization but explicitly honored and carried forward. Seventh confirmed instance of the recurring Construction/non-encroachment-clause template (Sec. 105).

**Notable positive institutional-design feature: EO 11280 (President's Committee on Mental Retardation, May 11 1966) staggers its 21 public/private members' terms across three cohorts** (expiring on the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd anniversaries, then rolling 3-year renewals) — a genuine anti-capture, institutional-memory-preserving design akin to a staggered corporate board, rarely seen among this stream's typical ad hoc study commissions. Consistent with this being a durable standing institution (no sunset) rather than a time-bound study commission.

**Companion-order structural comparison: EO 11258 (Water Pollution, Nov. 1965, 22.73%) and EO 11282 (Air Pollution, May 1966, 20.0%) each have a different structural gap.** EO 11258's third-party loan/grant-recipient extension is merely "encouraged," not required (a real accountability gap its air-pollution sibling doesn't share, since Air Pollution's scope is limited to direct federal facilities); EO 11282's Sec. 4(i) blanket facility-exemption power ("in extraordinary cases...required in the public interest") is broader and less narrowly gated than EO 11258's water-treatment-level-only exception. Both are genuine historical precursors to the federal environmental-regulatory apparatus that followed (NEPA 1969, EPA 1970, Clean Air Act Amendments 1970).

**EO 11263 (Ryukyu Islands administration, Dec. 20 1965) reflects the unique U.S.-administered-territory governance structure of Okinawa prior to 1972 reversion to Japan (20.0%)**: the U.S.-appointed High Commissioner holds backstop unilateral appointment power over the territory's Chief Executive whenever the local legislative body fails to elect one "within a reasonable time as determined by the High Commissioner" — an open-ended standard governing federal (U.S. military-administration) intervention in local self-governance.

**EO 11283 (International Cotton Institute, May 27 1966) has the most fully-negotiated immunities package in this stream's international-organization-designation family**, including a genuine self-waiver mechanism (Sec. 9: the Institute's own General Assembly may waive any granted immunity) and a member-veto-on-undesirable-persons clause (Sec. 7(d)) — both positive accountability features absent from this stream's briefer designation orders (EO 11225, EO 11227, EO 11277).

**Interest Equalization Tax exemption family gains two more entries, including a genuine reversal.** EO 11285 (June 10 1966) narrows country coverage (removing several Middle Eastern oil producers and the Kuwait-Saudi Arabia Neutral Zone) and supersedes EO 11224 (Batch 3 chunk 4) — notable for documented advance congressional notice, a modest accountability improvement over the family baseline. EO 11304 (Sept. 12 1966) does the opposite: it explicitly supersedes Section 3 of EO 11198 (Batch 3 chunk 3, 20.0%), reversing that order's clawback of the exemption from commercial banks and restoring their eligibility — while simultaneously adding new Sec. 2 anti-abuse exclusions (registered investment companies, Canadian pass-through shells) not present in the original EO 11175. A genuine "broadened for the affected actor-class, tightened against abuse" finding.

**Second confirmed instance of the staggered-cohort-term anti-capture design: EO 11287 (National Medal of Science, June 28 1966)**, following EO 11280 (Committee on Mental Retardation, this batch chunk 6) — confirms this is a recurring positive institutional-design template, not an isolated instance.

**Clean "mechanism preserved across a function transfer" finding: EO 11288 (Water Pollution, July 2 1966) is a near-verbatim re-issuance of EO 11258 (Batch 3 chunk 6, 22.73%)**, substituting the Secretary of the Interior for HEW throughout per Reorganization Plan No. 2 of 1966. Every flag finding carries forward unchanged and the numeric score is identical (22.73%) — the cleanest diff-aware confirmation this stream has produced.

**Two-tiered independent-Commission-feeding-Cabinet-Committee template diverges on the Construction clause within the same chunk.** EO 11301 (Libraries, Sept. 2 1966) is the 4th confirmed instance of the template but notably lacks the non-encroachment Construction clause present in siblings EO 11279 and EO 11280; EO 11306 (Rural Poverty, Sept. 27 1966), the 5th instance, does include it — confirming this safeguard is not uniformly applied even among near-identical sibling orders issued weeks apart.

**Matched pair of interagency-coordination orders confirms the Construction-clause template was an actively reused drafting pattern, not coincidence.** EO 11297 (Coordination of Federal Urban Programs, Aug. 11 1966, HUD) and EO 11307 (Coordination of Federal Programs Affecting Agricultural and Rural Area Development, Sept. 30 1966, USDA) use an almost word-for-word identical convene-meetings-plus-Construction-clause design for two different policy domains, seven weeks apart — 8th and 9th confirmed instances of this template.

**Sixth confirmed instance of the named-individual civil-service-exemption template, and the most retroactively-reaching yet found: EO 11303 ("Reginald C. Price," Sept. 12 1966)** waives a leave-of-absence limitation for a period from March 1954 through September 1957 — roughly nine to twelve years before this order's issuance — with zero stated rationale, not even the boilerplate "public interest" language used in earlier instances.

**EO 11305 (Panama Canal Zone governance delegation, Sept. 12 1966) contains two notable structural features**: Sec. 3.3(a)(7) delegates police power to enforce an entirely undefined "indecent or immoral conduct" standard with no further criteria, and Sec. 3.3(a)(5) vests magistrate (judicial-officer) appointment/removal power in the Governor, an executive territorial administrator — a structural blending of executive and judicial-appointment authority consistent with this stream's other Canal Zone/territorial-governance orders (EO 11171, Batch 3 chunk 2).

**Two RLA Emergency Boards this chunk (EO 11291, American Airlines; EO 11308, Pan American World Airways) both involve the same union (Transport Workers Union of America, AFL-CIO) across two different carriers** — both scored 0.0% per the standard template.

**EO 11310 (Emergency Preparedness Functions to the Attorney General, Oct. 11 1966) is the most structurally significant order of chunk 8 (37.5%).** Assigns the AG broad contingency-planning authority spanning alien-enemy detention, alien property seizure, security-standard adjustment, and penal-institution emergency use, bundled into a single planning assignment with repeated undefined "as appropriate" standards. Meaningfully checked only by Sec. 7's explicit reservation of actual implementation authority to the President — the order authorizes planning, not execution. Worth watching for parallel Reorganization-Plan-No.-1-of-1958 contingency assignments to other departments in later chunks.

**Three LMRA/Taft-Hartley Boards of Inquiry confirmed this chunk (EO 11314 General Electric, EO 11321 Union Carbide/Stellite, EO 11329 Pacific Coast Shipbuilders)**, each scoring 5.56% via the indefinite-continuation Flag 6 template first distinguished from RLA boards at EO 11181 (Batch 3 chunk 2) — the two labor-dispute-board templates remain cleanly and consistently distinguishable across now 4 confirmed LMRA instances.

**EO 11315 (Executive Assignment System, Nov. 17 1966) is a structurally significant civil-service reform — the direct institutional precursor to the 1978 Senior Executive Service (22.22%).** Notable for pairing a genuine political-appointment exemption category (Noncareer Executive Assignments) with real, multi-part safeguards: a numeric cap on excepted positions, periodic Commission review/revocation, and mandatory Federal Register disclosure — one of the better-checked exemption structures in this stream, comparable to the Canal Zone Merit System (EO 11171, Batch 3 chunk 2).

**EO 11322 (Southern Rhodesia sanctions, Jan. 5 1967) implements UN Security Council Resolution 232 — the first-ever mandatory UN sanctions regime — establishing a structural template (direct executive-order implementation of Security Council sanctions) that recurs extensively in later Cold War and post-Cold War chunks.** Enforcement is cleanly split three ways among State/Commerce/Treasury with a mandatory consultation requirement; noted purely as an early instance for future cross-referencing, without comment on the underlying policy.

**Second confirmed self-waiver-clause instance in international-organization designations: EO 11318 (European Space Research Organization, Dec. 5 1966), following EO 11283 (Cotton Institute, Batch 3 chunk 7)** — confirms this positive accountability feature is becoming a recurring drafting pattern, not a one-off.

**Diff-aware positive counter-example: EO 11333 (Marine Corps brigadier general retirement suspension, Mar. 7 1967) has an explicit termination date (June 29, 1968)**, unlike its close structural cousin EO 11284 (Navy rear admirals, Batch 3 chunk 7, indefinite duration) — a genuine sunset provision distinguishing otherwise near-identical personnel-suspension orders.

**EO 11328 (modifying EO 11198, Feb. 20 1967) is a genuine positive structural refinement over the IET family template**: Sec. 5 ties the order's duration to the underlying tax statute's own sunset date (26 U.S.C. 4911(d)) rather than leaving it indefinite — the first family member found to do so, dropping its score to 16.67% versus the family's typical 20.0%. Distinct from and independent of EO 11304's separate Canada/EO 11175-lineage reversal (Batch 3 chunk 7).

**Third confirmed staggered-cohort-term instance: EO 11330 (President's Council/Citizens Advisory Board on Youth Opportunity, Mar. 5 1967), after EO 11280 and EO 11287** — notable as the first instance of this positive design paired with a genuine sunset-clause absence, since it establishes an ongoing coordinating Council rather than a bounded study commission.

**Fourth and fifth confirmed tax-return-inspection-for-Congress instances this chunk (EO 11332 House, EO 11337 Senate, both Committees on Government Operations)** — both scored 5.56% per the established "direction matters" rule, consistent since EO 11217 (HUAC, Batch 3 chunk 4).

**Diff-aware structural continuity: EO 11326 (Ryukyu Islands air transport regulation, Feb. 13 1967) operates within the same U.S.-administered-territory command apparatus first heavily flagged at EO 10713 (this stream's highest-scoring order to date, Batch 1, 70.0%)**, confirming that occupied-territory governance structure remained fully active into 1967.

**EO 11360 (Selective Service Regulations overhaul, June 30 1967) is the most structurally significant order found in this stream's Selective Service lineage to date (38.89%, highest score of chunk 9).** Implements the Military Selective Service Act of 1967's restructuring of graduate/occupational draft deferments at the height of Vietnam War draft calls, including a new, criteria-free National Security Council role in identifying "critical skills" deferment-eligible occupations. Double-CRITICAL on Second/Third-Order Effects and Exemptions Architecture — evaluated strictly as a structural/governance matter without commentary on the underlying draft or war policy, consistent with this stream's neutral treatment of EO 11188, EO 11289, EO 11325, and EO 11350 (all this stream).

**New pattern identified: "long-running committee lineage," distinct from the EO 10530 hub-order disaggregation narrative. EO 11342 (Quetico-Superior Committee, Apr. 10 1967) consolidates and supersedes ten separate prior orders spanning five presidential administrations (EO 6783 of 1934, FDR, through EO 11031 of 1962, Kennedy)** — a clean, continuous re-issuance (not disaggregation) of a single advisory committee's charter, the deepest lineage chain found in this stream to date. Worth watching for recurrence among other long-standing natural-resource advisory committees.

**Clean "mechanism preserved through a full rewrite" finding: EO 11349 (May 1 1967) completely rewrites EO 11136 (this stream, 0.0%) in place** — renaming the Commission to a "Committee," restructuring Advisory Council terms into a staggered 1-2-year schedule — but every flag finding, including the continued incorporation of EO 11007's governance safeguards, carries forward unchanged, landing at an identical 0.0%.

**Carter's very first Executive Order.** EO 11967 (18.75%), issued on Inauguration Day, implements his signature blanket conditional pardon for Vietnam-era Selective Service Act violators, explicitly integrating with Ford's Presidential Clemency Board program (EO 11803, this stream, Batch 5 chunk 1, 12.5%) to close out the multi-year Vietnam-era clemency saga. **Carter's first touches on the landmark Ford-era intelligence-reform architecture (EO 11905, this stream, Batch 5 chunk 5, 33.33% CRITICAL).** EO 11984 (12.5%) abolishes the President's Foreign Intelligence Advisory Board (EO 11460, this stream, 31.25%), a standing outside intelligence-performance advisory board since 1969 -- notably, the genuine accountability mechanism (EO 11905's Intelligence Oversight Board) is untouched (Flag 7 ABSENT). **A landmark judicial-selection reform.** EO 11972 (US Circuit Judge Nominating Commission, 6.25%) establishes formal merit-selection commissions for federal circuit judge nominations for the first time -- 13 regional panels with genuine positive design throughout: mandatory public notice of vacancies, explicit diversity-composition requirements, a member-ineligibility conflict-of-interest safeguard, and a clean sunset.

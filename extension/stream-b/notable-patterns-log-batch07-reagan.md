## Batch 07 — Reagan (in progress)

### Chunk 1 (EO 12287-12315 excl. gaps/other-coder EOs, 25 EOs) — complete: the crude-oil decontrol landmark, a Carter-Reagan continuity surprise, and a wave of judicial/advisory-body reversals

**The long-flagged crude-oil decontrol order arrives, right on schedule.** EO12287 (Decontrol of Crude Oil and Refined Petroleum Products, 43.75%), Reagan's second Executive Order in office, is the exact order this stream anticipated at the close of the Carter batch (flagged via EO12153/EO12186/EO12189, this stream, Batch 6 Chunks 8-9). It immediately and unilaterally exempts the entire U.S. crude oil and refined petroleum products market from price and allocation controls dating to the 1973 oil embargo, with Sec.5 explicitly acknowledging and bypassing normal notice-and-comment rulemaking ("advance notice of and public procedure... would be contrary to the public interest") — the structural finding here (Flag 7 CRITICAL) is entirely about the no-public-process manner of the decontrol, not about concentrating any ongoing power, since the order extinguishes controls rather than creating new standing authority (Flag 1 ABSENT).

**A genuine continuity-of-government surprise: Reagan ratifies, rather than repudiates, Carter's most dramatic final-hours emergency actions.** EO12294 (Suspension of Litigation Against Iran, 50.0%) implements the Algiers Accords Claims Tribunal and, in Sec.8, explicitly "ratifies" EO12276 through EO12285 in full (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 12, several scoring 75.0% — this stream's all-time high). Despite the change in party control, the incoming administration formally affirmed its predecessor's sweeping emergency asset-transfer and claims-suspension architecture rather than contesting it — a clean, well-documented data point on executive continuity across hostile-succession transitions, distinct from every other reversal pattern in this chunk.

**A wave of reversals targeting Carter-era process and merit-selection bodies.** Three separate orders this chunk dismantle structures this stream had previously scored favorably: EO12298 (Lake Tahoe Region, 16.67%) terminates the Tahoe Federal Coordinating Council and revokes EO12247 (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 11, 31.25%) within five months of its issuance, devolving environmental review to the bistate Tahoe Regional Planning Agency; EO12299 (Presidential Advisory Board on Ambassadorial Appointments, 0.0%) terminates a board this stream flagged as "featuring genuine ineligibility/cooling-off safeguards and a clean sunset" (EO11970, this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 1, 5.56%); and most substantively, EO12305 (Termination of Certain Federal Advisory Committees, 50.0%) dismantles four judicial and quasi-judicial merit-selection nominating commissions, directly reversing the "landmark judicial-selection reform" thread this stream flagged at the very start of the Carter batch (EO11972, this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 1). Together these establish a genuine early-Reagan pattern: dismantling Carter-era oversight/merit-selection infrastructure regardless of each individual body's design quality.

**The Schedule-C politicization pattern recurs under a new administration.** EO12300 (Exceptions From the Competitive Service, 37.5%) converts USDA state-level program-administration positions to Schedule C political-appointee status and explicitly excludes them from Prohibited Personnel Practices protections (5 U.S.C. 2302) — a fresh instance of the exact pattern this stream tracked across three Ford-era orders (EO11839, EO11856, EO11887, this stream, Batch 5), now confirmed as a recurring structural phenomenon independent of party control.

**A second "well-designed despite enormous long-term stakes" landmark institution.** EO12301 (Integrity and Efficiency in Federal Programs, 25.0% CRITICAL) establishes the President's Council on Integrity and Efficiency — direct institutional predecessor of the statutory Council of the Inspectors General on Integrity and Efficiency (CIGIE, created 2008) — via a genuinely distributed 23-Inspector-General body rather than a concentrated single office, matching the pattern this stream identified in EO12009 (DOE, this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 2, 25.0% CRITICAL) and EO12286 (CERCLA/Superfund, this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 12, 31.25% CRITICAL).

**A New Federalism devolution thread emerges.** EO12314 (Federal Regional Councils, 12.5%) explicitly tasks ten restructured regional interagency councils with implementing "reform of the Federal aid system through block grants," "devolution of Federal programs," and "reduction... of Federal regulations" (Sec.2(c)) — the same federal-to-regional devolution logic as EO12298's Lake Tahoe reversal, now applied as an explicit government-wide policy vehicle. Worth tracking as a recurring ideological throughline across this batch.

**Routine templates and clean housekeeping:** two clean-sunset FACA bodies (Presidential Task Force on the Arts and Humanities, EO12308, 0.0%; President's Commission on Housing, EO12310, 6.25%, with a well-designed event-triggered rather than calendar-based sunset); a purely ceremonial Panama Canal Commission seal approval (EO12304, 0.0%); three GSP technical tariff amendments in quick succession (EO12302, EO12311, both 0.0%); continuation of the CIARDS/FSRDS benefits-conforming series (EO12289, 0.0%) and the Manual for Courts-Martial procedural-amendment chain (EO12306, EO12315, both 0.0%); and a direct factual determination fixing the Hostage Relief Act's statutory return-date trigger (EO12313, 16.67%).

### Chunk 2 (EO 12316-12343 excl. gaps/other-coder EOs, 25 EOs) — complete: the intelligence-community charter, the PFIAB-revival confirmation, and the maritime-interdiction landmark

**The foundational U.S. intelligence charter arrives.** EO12333 (United States Intelligence Activities, 37.5% CRITICAL) revokes Carter's EO12036 (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 4, 33.33% CRITICAL) and replaces it with the charter that — as amended in later decades — remains the core governing framework for the entire U.S. Intelligence Community as of 2026, over 44 years later. This is the most carefully-built accountability architecture this stream has encountered for an order of its stakes: explicit statutory Congressional oversight preservation, Attorney General approval required for any warrant-equivalent technique, a categorical assassination ban (Sec.2.11), human-subjects research protections, and a same-day companion oversight board (EO12334, this chunk). Real structural risk remains, however, in the DCI's broad budget/tasking concentration (Flag 1) and repeated open-textured collection standards ("reason to believe," "least intrusive... feasible," Flag 4) — genuine, if carefully bounded, risk factors inherent to intelligence governance that don't arise in this stream's other landmark "well-designed despite enormous stakes" institutions (EO12009 DOE 25.0%, EO12286 CERCLA 31.25%, EO12301 PCIE 25.0%).

**The PFIAB-revival cross-reference is confirmed.** EO12334 (President's Intelligence Oversight Board, 18.75%), issued the same day as EO12333, explicitly draws its Chairman "from among the membership of the President's Foreign Intelligence Advisory Board" — confirming PFIAB (abolished by Carter's EO11984, this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 1, 12.5%) had already been reestablished by this point, exactly as flagged for tracking at the close of the Carter batch. A genuine "who watches the watchers" finding: the Board tasked with catching unlawful intelligence activity is itself accountable only to the President (at-will removal, no independent Congressional reporting channel), a real structural asymmetry against the sophistication of EO12333's own safeguards.

**A landmark, legally consequential immigration-enforcement order.** EO12324 (Interdiction of Illegal Aliens, 50.0%) establishes the modern U.S. maritime migrant-interdiction program — later central to the Supreme Court's Sale v. Haitian Centers Council (1993) — authorizing the Coast Guard to stop, board, and return migrant vessels at sea. Its non-refoulement safeguard ("no person who is a refugee will be returned without his consent," Sec.2(c)(3)) specifies no formal screening procedure or standard of proof for making that determination at sea, a real and well-documented structural gap given the life-or-liberty stakes.

**A second consecutive exemption-stacking renewal, one step short of this stream's zombie-trap bar.** EO12327 (Exemption for Fort Allen, 50.0%) renews EO12244's four-statute environmental exemption (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 11, 50.0%) for a second consecutive year, now narrowed to Haitian nationals only. Scored identically to its predecessor given near-identical text. Not yet flagged with the zombie-trap sub-flag — a single renewal doesn't meet this stream's documented bar of confirmed indefinite historical persistence — but explicitly flagged to watch for a third renewal in upcoming chunks.

**A "replace, not just revoke" contrast to this chunk's reversal pattern.** Where EO12298, EO12299, and EO12305 (this stream, Batch 7 Chunk 1) simply terminated Carter-era process/equity bodies, two orders this chunk take a different approach: EO12320 (Historically Black Colleges and Universities, 12.5%) revokes Carter's EO12232 but replaces it with a *more* detailed multi-tier reporting structure (annual plans, midyear progress reports, year-end performance reports); and EO12336 (Task Force on Legal Equity for Women, 12.5%) revokes the President's Advisory Committee for Women's continuance but replaces it with a task force carrying its own substantive Attorney General regulatory-review mandate. Both are genuine positive-continuity counter-examples worth distinguishing from the straightforward-termination pattern.

**Landmark commission, textbook design.** EO12335 (National Commission on Social Security Reform, 12.5%) — the Greenspan Commission, whose work produced the Social Security Amendments of 1983 — features an explicit tripartisan-balance requirement built into all three appointing authorities' slates and a tight 30-day-post-report sunset. Its high real-world significance (Flag 9 CRITICAL) is entirely a function of the Commission's output, not any structural risk in the order itself, continuing this stream's recurring finding that policy impact and structural risk are independent axes.

**A documented environmental-policy reversal.** EO12342 (Environmental Safeguards for Animal Damage Control on Federal Lands, 16.67%) revokes Nixon's decade-old predator-poison restriction (EO11643, this stream, 11.11%) in a single sentence with no replacement safeguard provisions of its own — the order's title notwithstanding.

**Routine templates and clean housekeeping:** a restructured but structurally near-identical successor to Carter's CERCLA delegation order, adding a genuine new OMB pre-approval checkpoint for NCP amendments (EO12316, 31.25%, cross-referencing EO12291, not yet coded — flagged for future chunks); a River Basin Commissions mass wind-down continuing this batch's New Federalism devolution theme (EO12319, 16.67%); an OMB water-resources-report gatekeeping order with a genuine congressional-disclosure safeguard (EO12322, 25.0%); and continued technical chains for CIARDS conforming amendments (EO12326, 0.0%), Hostage Compensation Commission deadline extensions (EO12317, 0.0%), and Cuban/Haitian entrant program agency reassignment (EO12341, 16.67%).

### Chunk 3 (EO 12344-12370 excl. gaps/other-coder EOs, 25 EOs) — complete: the classification-policy reversal arrives, and two clean institutional-thread closures

**The long-flagged classification-policy reversal, and the richest diff-aware comparison in this stream to date.** EO12356 (National Security Information, 56.25%) revokes Carter's EO12065 (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 4, 27.78% CRITICAL) — exactly the order this stream anticipated tracking at the close of the Carter batch — and roughly doubles its predecessor's score through a genuine, well-documented, historically significant substantive reversal, not just a general posture shift. Sec.1.1(c) flips the presumption in doubtful cases from non-classification to "safeguarded as if it were classified pending a determination"; Sec.1.4(a) converts mandatory declassification scheduling into a discretionary "when it can be determined"; and Sec.3.4(f)(1) explicitly authorizes Glomar-style refusal to confirm or deny a record's existence in response to FOIA/Privacy Act requests. Real accountability architecture persists (ISOO oversight, sanctions provisions, NSC appeal process), but ISOO's own enforcement power is advisory rather than binding, and agencies may deny ISOO on-site access by citing "exceptional national security risk" — a genuine self-referential oversight gap.

**Two clean, well-planned institutional-thread closures.** EO12346 (Synthetic Fuels, 16.67%) fulfills, almost word-for-word, the automatic termination trigger this stream flagged when coding its predecessor EO12242 ("No new awards... after the date the President determines the... Corporation is established and fully operational," this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 11, 14.29%) — a clean confirmatory example of a well-designed sunset trigger firing exactly as written (with a real postscript worth tracking: the Synfuels Corporation itself was substantially defunded and largely dissolved by 1986). EO12357 (Sinai Support Mission wind-down, 16.67%) closes the multi-administration Sinai Support Mission thread this stream has tracked since Ford's EO11896 (this stream, 25.0%) through Carter's EO12150 (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 8, 0.0%), with an explicit trigger condition (MFO operational status), hard termination date, and clear successor-responsibility handoff to the State Department.

**A genuine positive-continuity case matching EO12364's earlier pattern.** EO12364 (Presidential Management Intern Program reconstitution, 12.5%) revokes Carter's EO12008 (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 2, 18.75% CRITICAL) but preserves nearly all of its predecessor's well-designed merit-protection safeguards — no automatic conversion right, competitive-selection nomination, OPM-gated conversion — while actually reducing the annual intake cap from 500 to 200. A second confirmed instance (after EO12320/EO12336, this stream, Batch 7 Chunk 2) of Reagan-era orders that revoke-and-replace rather than simply dismantle well-designed Carter-era structures.

**A concentrated White House-level real-property board with a real transparency gap.** EO12348 (Federal Real Property, 37.5%) vests a small, senior-White-House-staff Property Review Board with binding annual excess-property-disposal target-setting authority over every Executive agency, reporting only to the President with no external disclosure mechanism specified.

**Recurring Flag 5 pattern, third instance.** EO12362 (Overseas Employment, 20.0%) creates a noncompetitive civil-service conversion pathway for overseas-employee family members, scored identically to EO12257's CETA-conversion pattern (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 11, 20.0%) given equivalent OPM-gated mitigation architecture — a useful cross-batch confirmation that this stream's Flag 5 noncompetitive-conversion template recurs consistently regardless of the specific beneficiary population.

**Routine templates and clean housekeeping:** a codification of the celebrated Naval Nuclear Propulsion Program dual-hatted-director structure with genuine dual-Secretary-concurrence safeguards (EO12344, 25.0%); the Federal Procurement Reforms order that directly produced the still-governing Federal Acquisition Regulation via a diffuse, non-concentrated directive (EO12352, 12.5% — another "well-designed despite enormous stakes" institution, alongside EO12009 DOE and EO12286 CERCLA); a mandatory-safeguards-by-text Combined Federal Campaign framework (EO12353, 16.67%); three more FACA commissions with real downstream legislative legacies reflected purely through Flag 9 (Drunk Driving, Victims of Crime, Arts and Humanities — EO12358/EO12360/EO12367, 6.25% each); and continued technical GSP, Foreign Service, and Multinational Force/Observers reporting-chain maintenance.

### Chunk 4 (EO 12371-12396 excl. gaps/other-coder EOs, 25 EOs) — complete: a landmark devolution order, a genuine labor-rights curtailment, and a large zombie-prevention cleanup

**A landmark devolution order, the positive counterpart to this batch's concentration findings.** EO12372 (Intergovernmental Review of Federal Programs, 18.75% CRITICAL) terminated OMB Circular A-95's federal grant-review process and replaced it with a state-designed, state-run intergovernmental consultation framework — a genuine, large-scale transfer of federal review authority to state governments that remained the functional basis for federal-state grant consultation for decades. Continues this batch's New Federalism thread (EO12298, EO12314, EO12319, this stream) at government-wide scale, with a real (if modest) accountability safeguard requiring federal officials to explain their reasoning when unable to accommodate state/local concerns.

**A genuine, substantial federal labor-rights curtailment.** EO12391 (Partial Suspension of Federal Service Labor-Management Relations, 40.0%) suspends specified collective-bargaining provisions for DOD civilian employees stationed overseas wherever a matter would "substantially impair" treaty implementation with a host nation — vesting the Secretary of Defense with a final, unreviewable determination authority and no independent appeal mechanism for affected labor organizations. Explicitly authorized by the underlying statute's own national-security carve-out (5 U.S.C. 7103(b)(2)), which keeps this from CRITICAL, but the combination of finality and zero external review is a genuine structural concern.

**A second large-scale zombie-prevention cleanup, mirroring EO12258's pattern.** EO12379 (Termination of Boards, Committees, and Commissions, 0.0%) revokes 23 prior orders establishing inactive or completed bodies — several long-completed Railway Labor Act emergency boards, multiple FACA commissions, and notably EO12262, EO12275, and EO12285 (all this stream, all previously coded at 6.25% each) — a second confirmed instance of this administration proactively pruning genuinely completed structures rather than leaving them to lapse indefinitely (cf. EO12258, this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 11).

**A second confirmed instance of the positive presidential-reservation pattern.** EO12396 (Defense Officer Personnel Management, 12.5%) delegates substantial routine peacetime officer-personnel authorities to the Secretary of Defense, but explicitly walls off the more significant war/national-emergency-contingent authorities from unilateral exercise during an actual declared emergency absent specific Presidential direction, with mandatory National Emergencies Act accounting for any such use — extending the pattern this stream first tracked in EO11969 and EO12235 (both this stream, Carter batch) into a new military-personnel-delegation context, and a deliberate design choice avoiding this stream's tracked zombie-emergency-trap risk.

**Two clean cross-reference closures.** EO12381 (Delegation of Emergency Management Functions, 0.0%) conforms EO12148's (this stream, Batch 6 Chunk 8, 33.33% CRITICAL — the FEMA operational-buildout order) delegation structure to legislative changes, explicitly cross-referencing the River Basin Commissions wind-down (EO12319, this stream, Batch 7 Chunk 2, 16.67%) to justify one of its own revocations — a tidy example of two previously-tracked threads intersecting cleanly in a single conforming order.

**Routine templates and clean housekeeping:** three parallel same-day Rail Passenger Service Act Section 510 emergency boards for Northeast Rail Service Act Conrail-transfer disputes, featuring a more structured public-hearing-plus-final-offer-selection template than the standard RLA Section 10 boards (EO12384/EO12385/EO12386, all 0.0%); a long-lived National Security Telecommunications Advisory Committee with an explicit (if narrow) public-inspection exemption for submitted information (EO12382, 12.5%); a detailed Article 134 controlled-substance courts-martial amendment continuing this stream's tracked Manual for Courts-Martial chain (EO12383, 0.0%); and continued GSP technical-amendment, membership-expansion, and name-restoration housekeeping across several prior orders this stream has already coded.

### Chunk 5 (EO 12397-12422 excl. gaps/other-coder EOs, 25 EOs) — complete: this chunk's first CRITICAL finding, a genuine revoke-and-recreate sequence caught in real time, and a three-order Coast Guard consolidation

**This chunk's first CRITICAL finding, and a Supreme-Court-litigated eligibility restriction.** EO12404 (Charitable Fund-Raising, 66.67%) amends EO12353 (this stream, 16.67%) to add Combined Federal Campaign eligibility criteria excluding organizations that engage in "political activity or advocacy, lobbying, or litigation on behalf of parties other than themselves" — open-ended, discretion-laden language (Flag 4, CRITICAL) with no appeal process specified for excluded organizations (Flag 2) and a categorical gatekeeping architecture over workplace-solicitation access (Flag 11). This eligibility restriction was litigated to the Supreme Court in *Cornelius v. ### Chunk 6 (EO 12423-12448 excl. gaps/other-coder EOs, 25 EOs) — complete: this stream's first confirmed zombie-emergency-trap firing, two positive-design due-process cases, and a self-resolving three-extension commission chain

**The first confirmed zombie-emergency-trap sub-flag firing in the Reagan batch, and a landmark structural finding.** EO12444 (Continuation of Export Control Regulations, 42.86% CRITICAL) declares a national economic emergency under IEEPA to keep the entire export-control regulatory regime in force after the Export Administration Act of 1979 lapsed, with Sec.4 explicitly stating the order is a stopgap - "It is my intention to terminate this Order upon enactment into law of a bill reauthorizing" - and would "remain in effect until terminated." With the benefit of hindsight to 2026, this "temporary" emergency in fact persisted, through successive renewals and a 1994 re-declaration, for roughly 35 years, with the underlying export-control regime not placed on durable non-emergency statutory footing until the Export Control Reform Act of 2018. This is a direct structural cousin of EO12170's Iran-emergency renewal chain (Carter batch, this stream, 64.29% CRITICAL) - now confirmed in an entirely different policy domain (export/technology controls) - and the clearest, most historically-confirmed instance of this stream's zombie-emergency-trap pattern to date.

**Two genuine positive-design due-process cases in immediate succession.** EO12438 (Review of VA Basic Pay Increases, 0.0%) delegates a narrow pay-review authority to OPM bounded by three explicit, objective statutory criteria and a mandatory written-reasons disclosure to Congress for any disapproval; EO12448 (Regulations Related to Voiding or Rescission of Contracts, 0.0%) delegates bribery-related contract-voiding authority to agency heads but mandates at least four specific procedural protections (notice, opportunity to respond, a hearing with witness-confrontation rights, and a final written decision) directly in the order's text rather than leaving them to agency discretion. Both score 0.0% precisely because their delegations are paired with real, enumerated accountability safeguards rather than left open-ended - useful counterpoints to this chunk's EO12432 and this batch's EO12411 vague-standard findings.

**A second "bundled mandate, vague enforcement" government-wide coordination order.** EO12432 (Minority Business Enterprise Development, 50.0%) directs every federal agency with procurement or grant authority to build minority-business-development plans under a Cabinet Council coordination structure, but repeatedly qualifies obligations with "to the extent permitted by law and consistent with its primary mission" and "reasonable... participation" with no numeric targets or enforcement mechanism - structurally identical in form to EO12411's Work Space Management Reforms (this stream, Batch 7 Chunk 5, 50.0%), applied here to a policy domain (minority contracting) whose coordination architecture remained recognizable in federal procurement policy for decades.

**Three landmark commissions, distinguished by real-world outcome rather than structural design.** EO12433 (National Bipartisan Commission on Central America, 12.5%) - the Kissinger Commission - features an explicit, unusually precise bipartisan-balance requirement (no more than seven of twelve members from one party) and went on to significantly shape Reagan-era Central America policy and the Contra-aid debate. EO12435 (President's Commission on Organized Crime, 12.5%) conducted a multi-year national investigation whose findings substantially influenced federal organized-crime prosecution strategy through the mid-1980s. EO12439 (President's Task Force on Food Assistance, 12.5%) was structurally clean (tight 90-day reporting window, on-schedule sunset) but produced a controversial early-1984 report questioning the extent of U.S. hunger, drawing significant public criticism - a clean illustration of this stream's recurring finding that real-world policy impact (Flag 9) is an independent axis from structural design quality.

**A self-resolving three-extension commission chain, a useful contrast to EO12444.** The Grace Commission's life was extended three times within this stream's Batch 7 coverage - EO12398 (Batch 7 Chunk 5, 50.0%, membership expansion + extension), EO12429 (this chunk, 16.67%), and EO12447 (this chunk, 16.67%) - immediately before its actual January 1984 final report and wind-down. Observing all three extensions plus the actual on-schedule termination is a clean, low-inference contrast case to EO12444's genuinely indefinite renewal chain: repeated short extensions culminating in a real termination, rather than a stopgap that never resolved.

**Routine templates and clean housekeeping:** four more single-purpose FACA advisory bodies with clean sunsets (Women's Business Ownership, Private Sector Initiatives Council, Industrial Competitiveness Commission, and its own membership-expansion amendment - EO12426/12427/12428/12440, 6.25% or 0.0%); paired same-day CIARDS and Foreign Service retirement-system technical conforming amendments (EO12443/EO12446, both 0.0%); two stated-rationale deregulatory revocations (Reports of Identical Bids, Fuel Use Prohibitions - EO12430/EO12437, both 0.0%); continued GSP tariff and Executive Schedule technical-amendment chains; and a narrower-than-usual INTERPOL international-organization designation with explicit immunity carve-outs (EO12425, 0.0%).

### Chunk 7 (EO 12449-12473 excl. gaps/other-coder EOs, 25 EOs) — complete: a methodology self-correction on the zombie-emergency-trap finding, a landmark distributed-institution order, and a CRITICAL capital-punishment procedure order

### Chunk 1 (EO 12835-12861 excl. gaps, 25 EOs) — complete: the predicted revocations arrive immediately, three Cabinet-level coordinating councils are born, and China MFN gets human-rights strings attached

**Two Batch 8 watch items resolve within Chunk 1's first two orders.** EO12836 (16.67%), issued February 1, 1993 - Clinton's second week in office - revokes both EO12818 (this stream, 40.0%, Batch 8, the project-labor-agreement ban) and EO12800 (this stream, 50.0% CRITICAL, Batch 8, the union-dues-notice order with its self-determined 'national interest' exemption), confirming the Batch 8 closing summary's prediction almost immediately rather than 'early' as loosely forecast. EO12846 (50.0%) confirms the second predicted continuity: the Yugoslavia sanctions chain (EO12808/EO12810/EO12831, this stream, Batch 8) continues seamlessly into the new administration, escalating further by blocking all Serbian/Montenegrin commercial and industrial property - not just government assets - implementing UN Security Council Resolution 820.

**A cross-administration continuity finding in the self-exemption pattern.** EO12844 (Federal Use of Alternative Fueled Vehicles, 37.5%) contains a Sec.8 carve-out letting DOD, Treasury, and DOJ self-determine how much of the order applies to their own activities - textually identical in structure to EO12759's Sec.4(c) (this stream, 42.86%, Batch 8, Federal Energy Management). This is useful confirmation that the self-exemption pattern this stream has repeatedly documented is a durable feature of federal-facilities/procurement orders independent of which party controls the White House, not an artifact of one administration's drafting habits.

**Three Cabinet-level coordinating councils, mirroring the NSC model.** EO12835 (National Economic Council, 16.67%) and EO12859 (Domestic Policy Council, 16.67%) are structurally near-identical - both chaired by the President, both coordinating a large Cabinet-level membership, both explicitly preserving existing agencies' traditional roles (a deliberate non-cannibalization design choice). Together with the pre-existing National Security Council, these three orders complete the tripartite coordinating-council architecture that still structures the modern White House as of 2026.

**A landmark, self-limiting human-rights-conditionality order.** EO12850 (China MFN Conditions, 50.0%) is this chunk's most historically significant order: it conditions 1994 MFN renewal on binding freedom-of-emigration and prison-labor preconditions plus five non-binding 'overall, significant progress' human-rights factors (Tiananmen-detainee accounting, Tibetan cultural protection, and others). This deliberately self-constraining order became the central flashpoint of 1993-94 U.S.-China policy - and was itself abandoned roughly a year later when Clinton formally delinked human rights from China's MFN status, a rare instance of a major foreign-policy order being explicitly reversed by its own author within the same term. Flagged for follow-up when this stream reaches the delinkage order.

**Twin 'Reinventing Government' downsizing orders that still centralize gatekeeping.** EO12838 (Termination and Limitation of Federal Advisory Committees, 50.0%) and EO12861 (Elimination of One-Half of Executive Branch Internal Regulations, 50.0%) both cut government apparatus (one-third of non-statutory FACA committees; half of internal regulations) while concentrating all exemption/approval authority in OMB under undefined standards ('compelling considerations,' 'greatest improvement in productivity') - a structurally interesting finding that net-downsizing initiatives are not automatically low-scoring under this methodology when they centralize discretionary gatekeeping power.

**A governance-design gap explicitly corrected.** EO12852 (President's Council on Sustainable Development, 12.5%) revokes EO12737 (this stream, 0.0%, Batch 8), a commission this stream specifically flagged as lacking any termination clause at all - and its replacement builds in an explicit, dated 2-year sunset (Sec.4(b)) from day one. A rare, clean example of a structural flaw being identified and fixed at the very next opportunity.

**A landmark environmental-transparency order.** EO12856 (Federal Compliance With Right-to-Know Laws and Pollution Prevention Requirements, 40.0%) extends EPCRA Toxics Release Inventory public reporting - long a private-industry-only obligation - to federal facilities themselves, with guaranteed public access to the resulting reports (Sec.5-508) and a genuine dated sunset for its own reporting sub-requirement (Sec.4-402, expiring 2001). A strong 'government leads by example' precedent.

### Chunk 2 (EO 12862-12888 excl. gaps, 25 EOs) — complete: the landmark regulatory-review order arrives, a fourth coordinating council closes the loop, and Haiti sanctions pivot toward individual designation

**The single most historically significant order this stream has coded to date.** EO12866 (Regulatory Planning and Review, 30.0% CRITICAL on flag 9) revokes and replaces EO12291 (multi-coder corpus, ~54.6%) and EO12498 (this stream, 75.0%, Batch 7 — formerly this stream's all-time high-water mark before EO12656's 83.33%). EO12866, as later amended, remains the core OIRA regulatory-review framework governing the entire federal government as of 2026. Notably, its own score is moderate rather than critical across most flags: it introduces a specific, numerically-anchored $100-million "significant regulatory action" threshold (Sec.3(f), replacing an undefined predecessor standard), a genuine public-disclosure regime for OIRA-agency communications (Sec.6(b)(4)), and an explicit non-preemption-of-judicial-review clause (Sec.10) — a case study in an order being simultaneously the most consequential in the corpus and one of the more carefully-designed, precisely because it improves on its predecessor's transparency and judicial-review posture rather than concentrating power further.

**The 'big four' coordinating-council architecture is completed.** EO12881 (National Science and Technology Council, 33.33%) is the fourth Cabinet-level coordinating council this batch, joining the NSC and this batch's National Economic Council/Domestic Policy Council (both this stream, 16.67%, Chunk 1). Notable for a genuine consolidation feature (Sec.5(a)): it absorbs oversight of three pre-existing science-policy bodies (the Federal Coordinating Council for Science, Engineering, and Technology, the National Space Council, and the National Critical Materials Council) rather than layering a new body atop them. Issued the same day, EO12882 restructures PCAST as NSTC's private-sector counterpart — the third distinct structural intervention on that same advisory body within roughly five months (lapse at EO12768's June 30 expiration → reestablishment at EO12869's September 30 revival → full restructuring here on November 23), a genuine institutional-churn data point.

**The largest omnibus FACA order this stream has tracked.** EO12869 (33.33%) continues ten unrelated standing committees, reestablishes the lapsed PCAST, revokes six separate prior orders, and supersedes EO12774 (this stream, 30.0%, Batch 8) — the prior omnibus cleanup order itself. Third and largest installment in the recurring omnibus-FACA-cleanup pattern (EO12692 → EO12774 → EO12869, all this stream).

**Haiti sanctions design evolves toward individual designation.** EO12872 (Blocking Property of Persons Obstructing Democratization in Haiti, 50.0%) shifts from blocking government property (EO12775, this stream, 25.0%, Batch 8) or a country-wide trade embargo (EO12779, this stream, 41.67%, Batch 8) to targeting individually-identified obstructionist persons under open-ended trigger categories with no published designation list — a structural precursor to the modern OFAC Specially Designated Nationals List approach.

**A rare non-state-actor sanctions target.** EO12865 (Prohibiting Certain Transactions Involving UNITA, 33.33%) bans arms and petroleum sales to Angola's UNITA rebel faction, implementing UN Security Council Resolution 864 — one of the few sanctions orders in this corpus targeting an armed faction rather than a national government.

**Emergency-scope refinement, not just renewal.** EO12867 (16.67%) cleanly terminates the IEEPA emergency that had substituted for lapsed Export Administration Act authority once Congress closed the statutory gap (PL 103-10) — a positive zombie-emergency counter-example. Its same-day companion, EO12868 (33.33%), declares a narrower, purpose-specific weapons-proliferation emergency rather than simply re-upping the old broad declaration — together demonstrating deliberate emergency-scope narrowing rather than indefinite continuation.

**A first: federal-sector union presidents seated on a formal advisory council.** EO12871 (Labor-Management Partnerships, 33.33%) seats the presidents of AFGE, NFFE, and NTEU directly on the new National Partnership Council alongside OPM, OMB, FLRA, and FMCS officials — the first instance in this stream's corpus of union leadership holding designated seats on an executive-branch coordinating body. Carries a genuine 2-year dated sunset (Sec.1(d)(3)).

**Continuing threads.** EO12888 (25.0%), the seventh Manual for Courts-Martial installment this stream has tracked (EO12473→12484→12550→12586→12767→this order), formalizes military-judge professional-discipline procedures with a "clear and convincing evidence" standard but an explicit no-appeal finality clause for Judge Advocate General decisions (Sec.1(c)(6)(B)) — a narrowly-scoped but genuine oversight-preemption mechanism. EO12878 (Bipartisan Commission on Entitlement Reform, 12.5%) — the historically significant Kerrey-Danforth Commission — requires a 3/5 supermajority for recommendations while guaranteeing dissenting views are preserved (Sec.2(b)), a more rigorous consensus/dissent-preservation design than most commissions this stream has tracked; amended eight weeks later by the purely technical EO12887 to expand membership 30→32.

### Chunk 3 (EO 12889-12914 excl. gaps 12897, 25 EOs) — complete: NAFTA implementation, twin landmark equity orders with matching exemption clauses, and the Haiti chain's most severe escalation yet

**Two landmark equity-policy orders issued four days apart share an identical discretionary escape valve.** EO12892 (Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing, 25.0%) and EO12898 (Environmental Justice, 42.86% CRITICAL) both establish foundational, still-contested federal equity frameworks — AFFH remains a live HUD regulatory battleground repealed and reinstated across administrations, while EO12898's environmental-justice policy was targeted for elimination early in a later Republican administration and reaffirmed/expanded by a later Democratic one (EO14096, 2023). Both orders contain a textually near-identical "Petitions for Exemptions" clause (EO12892 Sec.6-605, EO12898 Sec.6-605) letting any agency head petition the President for an undefined exemption from the order's requirements — a genuine, shared discretionary-exemption architecture spanning two of this administration's most consequential equity initiatives. EO12898 additionally scores CRITICAL on flag 9 given its three-decade-plus durability and its "to the greatest extent practicable" qualifier appearing dozens of times throughout the text (flag 4).

**The Haiti sanctions chain reaches its most severe escalation.** EO12914 (50.0%) extends blocking not just to Haitian military/police officers and coup participants but explicitly to their undefined "immediate families" — a genuine overbreadth concern (family members blocked by association, not conduct) — while adding a near-total flight ban. This is the fourth and most severe installment in the chain this stream has tracked (EO12775 25.0% → EO12779 41.67% → EO12872 50.0% → EO12914 50.0%, all this stream), issued roughly three months before the U.S. intervention that restored Aristide.

**A historically significant investigative commission with an ironic accountability gap.** EO12891 (Advisory Committee on Human Radiation Experiments, 50.0%) investigates Cold War-era nonconsensual human radiation experiments (the Hanford "Green Run" test, Oak Ridge, Dugway, Los Alamos), leading to a landmark 1995 report and lasting human-subjects-research reforms — yet Sec.4(a) explicitly exempts the Committee from FACA's standard annual report-to-Congress requirement, a notable tension between a body investigating government misconduct and its own transparency design.

**The self-exemption pattern extends to a third policy domain.** EO12906 (National Spatial Data Infrastructure, 33.33%) exempts DOD/DOE/intelligence-community activities from coverage based on each agency's own self-determination (Sec.7(b)) — the third confirmed instance of this stream's tracked self-exemption pattern (after EO12759 federal energy management and EO12844 alternative-fueled vehicles, both this stream), now applied to geospatial data policy, itself a foundational framework that led to the 2018 Geospatial Data Act.

**"Super 301" revives aggressive unilateral trade enforcement.** EO12901 (16.67%) mandates (not merely authorizes) USTR investigation of every identified "priority foreign country practice" within 21 days — a non-discretionary trigger unusual for this stream's typically discretionary delegation template — reviving a mechanism that became internationally controversial in 1990s U.S.-Japan and U.S.-China trade disputes.

**Litigation-driven revocation.** EO12913 (16.67%) revokes EO12582 (this stream, 0.0%, Reagan-era) retroactively to 1987, explicitly citing the Ninth Circuit's Matter of Reyes decision — a rare instance in this corpus of a formal EO revocation driven by adverse appellate litigation rather than policy preference, with a careful reliance-interest savings clause for those already naturalized under the defective order.

**NAFTA implementation cluster.** EO12889 (20.0%) implements NAFTA's U.S. entry into force across six bundled mechanisms (Secretariat, panel-decision acceptance, textile safeguards, procurement waivers, patent-notice waivers); EO12904 (0.0%) is the companion IOIA designation for four NAFTA-created institutions (CEC, CLC, BECC, NADBank).

EO12902 (25.0%) revokes EO12759 (this stream, 42.86%, Batch 8) while preserving three of its sections, adding numeric audit-completion targets and an Energy-Secretary-approved (rather than self-determined) waiver mechanism — a modest accountability improvement over the pure self-exemption pattern. ### Chunk 4 (EO 12915-12940 excl. gap 12937, 25 EOs) — complete: the Haiti chain reaches its most severe order and then a clean resolution, the WMD nonproliferation framework is born, and a "clean termination" turns out not to be so clean

**The Haiti sanctions chain reaches its structural peak, then resolves cleanly.** This chunk contains four more Haiti installments (EO12917 50.0% trade embargo, EO12920 50.0% financial-transfer ban, EO12922 66.67% CRITICAL) before the entire eight-order chain is cleanly revoked at EO12932 (16.67%) following the September 1994 intervention (activated by the reserve-mobilization order EO12927, this chunk) and restoration of Aristide. EO12922 is the single most severe finding in this stream's Haiti tracking to date: it blocks the property of "any Haitian national resident in Haiti" -- nationality/residence alone, with no individualized conduct determination -- a qualitatively broader mechanism than any predecessor in the chain (EO12775 25.0% -> EO12872 50.0% -> EO12914 50.0% -> EO12917 50.0% -> EO12920 50.0% -> EO12922 66.67% CRITICAL, all this stream). EO12932's clean, comprehensive eight-order revocation is nonetheless a strong positive zombie-emergency counter-example, joining EO12535 (South Africa) and EO12771 (Kuwait) in this stream's tracked "clean resolution" examples.

**A celebrated clean termination turns out to be temporary.** EO12923 (50.0%) re-declares the IEEPA export-control emergency after the Export Administration Act again lapsed -- the third instance of this stream's tracked EAA-lapse/emergency-substitute pattern. This complicates a Chunk 3 finding: EO12867 (this stream, 16.67%) had been celebrated as a clean, positive termination of the original 1990 version of this same emergency, but that termination lasted only about nine months before Congress's EAA-reauthorization stalemate recurred. EO12923 was itself revoked and replaced by a near-identical successor, EO12924 (50.0%), just seven weeks later -- and EO12924 notably drops EO12923's explicit stated intent to terminate upon EAA reauthorization, a subtle weakening of the self-limiting language. A parallel, faster cycle plays out with the WMD-proliferation emergency: EO12930 (16.67%) re-declares EO12868's emergency on almost its exact one-year anniversary (demonstrating the National Emergencies Act's annual-renewal requirement functioning as intended), only to be superseded seven weeks later by the comprehensive EO12938.

**A foundational, three-decade nonproliferation sanctions architecture is born.** EO12938 (60.0% CRITICAL) establishes the WMD Proliferation sanctions framework whose basic architecture, as subsequently amended, underlies much of the modern U.S. nonproliferation sanctions practice against Iran, North Korea, Syria, and other programs. Structurally notable: the same official (the Secretary of State) holds the sanctions-triggering determination authority (flag 1), a self-administered waiver of the very sanctions that determination triggers (flag 2, Sec.5(b)), and open-ended exemption authority (flag 11, Sec.4(d)) -- three separate discretionary powers concentrated in one office with no independent check specified in the order's own text.

**A durable, forward-looking delegation buried in a single-crisis order.** EO12918 (50.0%) responds to the Rwandan arms embargo (UNSCR 918, issued during the early weeks of the Rwandan genocide) but its Sec.5 delegates standing authority to State/Commerce to implement ANY future UN Security Council arms embargo without requiring a new Executive order each time -- a durable expansion of executive sanctions-implementation authority that outlives the Rwanda crisis specifically.

**A genuinely enforceable worker-protection order -- a positive design counter-example.** EO12933 (16.67%) requires successor federal building-service contractors to offer predecessor employees a right of first refusal, backed by real Department of Labor investigation/adjudication authority and up-to-3-year contractor debarment (Sec.5) -- and Sec.6 explicitly preserves, rather than disclaims, APA judicial review of the Secretary's final decisions. A notable positive counter-example to this stream's more common toothless-aspirational-order pattern.

**The Yugoslavia/Bosnia chain adds a territorial/associational blocking trigger.** EO12934 (50.0%) expands EO12808's emergency (this stream, 33.33%, Batch 8) to block all entities "organized or located" in Bosnian-Serb-controlled territory -- structurally similar to (though geographically narrower than) EO12922's Haiti-nationality approach, following Bosnian Serb rejection of the proposed peace settlement.

**A comprehensive, still-operative Defense Production Act framework.** EO12919 (50.0%) consolidates ten predecessor DPA delegation orders spanning 1939-1991 into a single nine-part framework, including a subpoena-power delegation (Sec.902(b)(2)); its basic structure underlies decades of subsequent DPA invocations including COVID-19-era production mandates.

**Continuing threads.** EO12936 (16.67%) is the eighth tracked Manual for Courts-Martial installment, adding a classified-evidence protective-order mechanism structurally analogous to the civilian Classified Information Procedures Act. EO12940 (50.0%) continues this stream's cross-administration Schedule-C civil-service-politicization pattern (previously tracked under Carter, this stream) into the Clinton administration, notably without a stated grandfather clause. ### Chunk 5 (EO 12941-12967 excl. gaps 12958/12961, 25 EOs) — complete: the foundational terrorism-sanctions order arrives, the Iran embargo is born, and a court strikes down an EO for the first time in this stream's tracking

**The single most historically consequential order this stream has coded to date.** EO12947 (Prohibiting Transactions With Terrorists Who Threaten To Disrupt the Middle East Peace Process, 66.67% CRITICAL) establishes the direct structural predecessor of the entire modern individual/entity terrorist-designation and asset-blocking architecture -- later dramatically expanded post-9/11 by EO13224 into the Specially Designated Global Terrorist regime still governing U.S. counterterrorism financing decades later. **The modern Iran sanctions architecture is born in two steps.** EO12957 (50.0%) opens with a narrow petroleum-development-contract ban; seven weeks later EO12959 (66.67% CRITICAL) expands it into a comprehensive trade/investment embargo with an extraterritorial foreign-affiliate oil-transaction reporting requirement (Sec.3) -- a significant reach beyond this stream's typical domestic-nexus sanctions template. **A rare judicial invalidation.** EO12954 (the "striker replacement ban," 33.33% CRITICAL) was struck down by the D.C. Circuit in Chamber of Commerce v. Reich (1996) as preempted by the NLRA -- despite being one of this stream's more structurally well-designed labor orders (a genuine agency-override mechanism at Sec.3(b) and an explicit judicial-review-preservation clause at Sec.10, both flags ABSENT). This is the first confirmed instance in this stream's corpus of an Executive order being invalidated by the federal courts rather than simply revoked by a later administration -- a useful reminder that this methodology's flags measure internal governance-architecture design, not constitutional/preemption survivability.

**A recurring FACA-transparency-carve-out drafting template is confirmed.** EO12963 (Presidential Advisory Council on HIV/AIDS, 50.0%) contains a FACA annual-report-to-Congress exemption clause textually identical to EO12891's (this stream, 50.0%, Chunk 3, Human Radiation Experiments Committee) -- confirming this is a standard HHS/OMB advisory-committee drafting template rather than a one-off choice. **A second confirmed instance of a supermajority/dissent-preservation commission template.** EO12964 (Commission on U.S.-Pacific Trade and Investment Policy, 12.5%) matches EO12878's (this stream, 12.5%, Chunk 2) 3/5-supermajority-with-guaranteed-dissent design exactly -- suggesting a deliberate, reusable institutional template for this administration's high-stakes policy commissions.

**Continuing threads.** EO12955 (50.0%) is the sixteenth consecutive EURATOM extension, notably realigning the renewal cycle to the calendar year, superseding EO12903 (this stream, 50.0%, Chunk 3). EO12960 (16.67%) is the ninth tracked MCM amendment installment, adding a new government interlocutory-appeal right for classified-discovery rulings. ### Chunk 6 (EO 12968-12992, 25 EOs, contiguous) — complete: the foundational security-clearance framework, a second designation-list sanctions target, and a recurring FACA carve-out confirmed a third time

**The foundational government-wide security-clearance architecture.** EO12968 (Access to Classified Information, 50.0% CRITICAL) establishes the uniform personnel-security-clearance program still governing federal clearance holders three decades later. Its otherwise genuinely robust due-process review procedures (Sec.5.2 -- written explanation, document production, right to counsel, an outside-majority appeal panel) are undercut by Sec.5.2(d)'s unreviewable, agency-head-certified escape valve -- a structural echo of this stream's earlier "unreviewable discretion" finding at EO12807 (Batch 8). Notable also for explicit sexual-orientation and mental-health nondiscrimination protections (Sec.3.1(c)-(e)), genuinely progressive for its era.

**A second designation-list sanctions architecture.** EO12978 (Blocking Assets and Prohibiting Transactions With Significant Narcotics Traffickers, 50.0%) -- the "Clinton List" -- applies the same executive-branch-only designation model pioneered at EO12947 (this stream, 66.67% CRITICAL, Chunk 5, terrorism) to Colombian narcotics trafficking, becoming the direct predecessor of the modern OFAC Specially Designated Narcotics Trafficker program still used against cartels globally.

**A third confirmed instance of the recurring FACA carve-out template.** EO12975 (National Bioethics Advisory Commission, 33.33%) contains the same FACA annual-report-to-Congress exemption clause found at EO12891 (this stream, 50.0%, Chunk 3) and EO12963 (this stream, 50.0%, Chunk 5) -- now clearly established as a standard HHS/OMB drafting convention. NBAC is also historically notable as the direct institutional follow-through on EO12891's Human Radiation Experiments Committee findings, with human gene-patenting flagged as a first-priority issue years before the genomics-policy debates of the following decade.

**A federal-procurement-compliance enforcement trio is completed, with a notable regression.** EO12989 (Immigration compliance in procurement, 33.33%) joins EO12933 (this stream, 16.67%, Chunk 4) and EO12954 (this stream, 33.33% CRITICAL, Chunk 5) as the third installment of this stream's tracked contractor-compliance-enforcement pattern -- but is the only one of the three to contain an explicit non-reviewability clause (Sec.4(b): "The Attorney General's determination shall not be reviewable in the debarment proceedings"), a genuine structural regression on this specific dimension relative to its two companion orders.

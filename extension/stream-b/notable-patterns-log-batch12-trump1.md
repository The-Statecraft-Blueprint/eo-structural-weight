### Chunk 1

**The corpus's most legally contested first-month sequence: a "cure the litigation defect" successor order.** EO13780 (Travel Ban 2.0, 50.0% CRITICAL, this chunk's highest score) revokes EO13769 (not stream-b scored; other coder `claude-church-bells-v1`: 72.73%, one of that coder's highest recorded scores) and is drafted specifically to cure the due-process and religious-discrimination defects identified in nationwide federal-court injunctions against its predecessor — dropping Iraq from the designated-country list, adding eight categorical exceptions, and removing the indefinite Syria-specific suspension. This is a genuinely novel diff-aware pattern this stream has not previously tracked: a successor order responding directly and explicitly to adverse judicial review of its own predecessor, rather than to a change in underlying policy or administration.

**A rapid, complete dismantling of a four-year, multi-order Obama-era compliance architecture.** EO13782 (Revocation of Federal Contracting EOs, 12.5%, CRITICAL Flag9) revokes EO13673 (this stream: 40.0%, Chunk 8 of the Obama batch, at the time the corpus's most structurally dense federal-procurement-labor order), Section 3 of EO13683 (this stream: 6.25%), and EO13738 (this stream: 0.0%) in a single three-section order — unwinding in one action a "Fair Pay and Safe Workplaces" compliance regime that took the Obama administration four years and four sequential orders (EO13658→EO13672→EO13673→EO13706, tracked across Obama Chunks 8-9) to construct.

**The single most comprehensive rollback of Obama-era climate-governance architecture this stream has tracked, executed in one 8-section order.** EO13783 (Promoting Energy Independence and Economic Growth, 43.75%, two CRITICAL flags) outright revokes EO13653 (this stream: 43.75%, Obama's 2013 Climate Change Preparedness order) — notably, EO13653 had only recently been AMENDED, not revoked, by EO13693 (this stream: 44.44%, Chunk 9 of the Obama batch), making this the first full revocation of EO13653 this stream has tracked — while also disbanding the Interagency Working Group on Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases (withdrawing six technical support documents), rescinding CEQ climate-NEPA guidance, and directing review of the Clean Power Plan.

**A novel "regulatory-bookkeeping" incentive-distortion finding, distinct from the familiar Zombie Emergency pattern.** EO13771 (the "2-for-1" order, 40.0%, CRITICAL Flag5) mandates that agencies repeal two existing regulations for every one new regulation, regardless of the substantive merits of either the new rule or the ones selected for repeal — creating an incentive to "bank" low-cost, easily-repealable rules against future rulemaking needs independent of whether those rules serve legitimate public-protection functions. Companion order EO13777 (Enforcing the Regulatory Reform Agenda, 44.44%, CRITICAL Flag3) operationalizes EO13771 across every federal agency just three weeks later, while explicitly layering atop — without amending or revoking — both EO12866 (this stream: 30.0%, 1993 Clinton) and EO13563 (this stream: 25.0%, 2011 Obama), the corpus's most extensive regulatory-review-framework-stacking order to date.

**Two DOJ succession orders within 71 days, an unusually fast churn rate.** EO13775 (Feb 9, revoking EO13762: 6.25%, Obama Chunk 11) was itself revoked by EO13787 (Mar 31) just seven weeks later — both routine succession()-template orders scoring the fixed 6.25% (1/16) value, but the rapid double-revision is a notable administrative-transition artifact worth tracking as this batch progresses.

**A first-day order whose stated goal was never achieved, paralleling this stream's Obama Chunk 1 finding.** EO13765 (Minimizing the Economic Burden of the ACA Pending Repeal, 38.89%, CRITICAL Flag4) grants sweeping agency waiver/delay discretion "pending" an anticipated ACA repeal that, as a matter of well-documented subsequent history, was never legislatively completed — echoing this stream's EO13492 Guantanamo-closure finding (Obama Chunk 1) of a stated policy deadline/goal that did not match subsequent political reality.

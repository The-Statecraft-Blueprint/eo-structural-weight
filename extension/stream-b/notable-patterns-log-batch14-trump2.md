### Chunk 1 (EO14147-14171, 25 EOs)

- **A new "redundant same-day revocation" pattern, unprecedented in this stream's 14-batch tracking.** EO14148 (Initial Rescissions of Harmful Executive Orders and Actions) revokes 55 Executive Orders in one instrument on Inauguration Day. SIX separate later orders issued that same day (EO14154, EO14155, EO14159, EO14163, EO14168, EO14171) then independently re-revoke a subset of those same predecessors: EO14154 re-revokes 10 of its 11 targets; EO14155 re-revokes 1; EO14159 re-revokes 4; EO14163 re-revokes 1 (EO14013, now revoked THREE separate times in one day -- a new record for most-redundantly-revoked order); EO14168 re-revokes 5; EO14171 re-revokes 1 (while also reinstating-with-amendment a different, older order). No comparable redundant-revocation clustering appears anywhere in Batches 1-13 (Eisenhower through Biden) -- this appears to be a genuine artifact of an unusually large, uncoordinated single-day drafting operation (12 EOs signed January 20, 2025 alone in this chunk) rather than an isolated error.
- **Two orders in this single chunk set a new corpus-wide structural-weight high-water mark, surpassing Biden's EO14110 (70.0%, the prior record).** EO14154 (Unleashing American Energy) scores 71.43% (three CRITICAL flags: Bundling, Vague Enforcement, Second/Third-Order Effects) and EO14171 (Restoring Accountability to Policy-Influencing Positions / Schedule F reinstatement) scores 83.33% -- the highest score in this stream's entire corpus to date, driven by four CRITICAL flags (Power Concentration, Accountability Gaps, Vague Enforcement, Second/Third-Order Effects) concentrated in just seven applicable flags. EO14171's "faithfully implement administration policies... [or] dismissal" standard is the most consequential vague-enforcement finding this stream has recorded.
- **A new "individual-targeting via EO" technique.** EO14152 directly names 50 private citizens and former officials and orders their security clearances revoked "effective immediately" with no due process, appeal, or independent review -- a form of individualized executive action this stream has not previously catalogued in any of the 3,546 records coded before this chunk.
- **A novel "self-displacing" legal-authority clause.** EO14158 (DOGE) Section 4(c) states the order itself "displaces all prior executive orders and regulations... that might serve as a barrier" to a new agency's data-access mandate -- an open-ended, self-authorizing preemption of unnamed prior legal protections, distinct from every amendment/revocation technique this stream has tracked to date.
- **The most complex predecessor-disposition technique yet catalogued.** EO14171 simultaneously reinstates-with-seven-textual-amendments one predecessor (EO13957, "Schedule F," Trump 1st term) while redundantly revoking a second, already-dead predecessor (EO14003) in the same instrument -- combining two previously-separate techniques (reinstatement-with-amendment, and redundant revocation) for the first time.
- **Chunk-wide bundling intensity is markedly higher than any prior batch's opening chunk.** Five orders in this 25-EO chunk (EO14148, EO14153, EO14154, EO14159, EO14168) scored CRITICAL on Bundling alone, each spanning seven or more distinct policy domains in a single instrument -- more CRITICAL-bundling orders than appeared in some entire prior-administration batches.

This is distinct from mere revocation or amendment: these orders treat earlier same-administration EOs as reusable legal building blocks, a genuinely new intra-batch dependency pattern not observed within any single chunk of Batches 1-13.
